              Hi! I'm Eric, a first-year PhD student in Computer Science at
              Northeastern University advised by
              Terra Blevins and
              David Smith.
            

            

              My research is in multilingual Natural Language Processing (NLP).
              I view translation as a fundamentally interpretive act, and as
              Large Language Models (LLMs) are increasingly being trusted for
              that interpretation I'm passionate about ensuring translations
              are faithful and useful to humans, and as language model-based
              tools spread through society, ensuring that the benefits of the
              technology are felt equally across languages. Specifically, I'm
              interested in:
            

            
    
              
  • 
                Using the linguistic relationships between languages to inform
                the development of multilingual AI.
              
    • 
              
  • 
                Working with humans to isolate the gaps between perceived and
                actual performance of multilingual NLP systems.
              
    • 
            

            

              In 2022 I graduated from the University of Maryland with bachelor
              degrees in Computer Science and Chinese, where I was advised by
              Marine Carpuat
              and
