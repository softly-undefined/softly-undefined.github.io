    Title: Records of Strange Events

    Text:
    King Qiao of the Sima clan, a person from Xiangzhou, wore silver bracelets (or possibly a silver mirror) and other ornaments on his face. He bought wine and meat. One night, he dreamed of a Taoist priest who complained to King Qiao about seeking the bracelets (or other items). Upon checking, he immediately returned them.     ○ In the second year of Jianwu, Zhang Kai returned from the wilderness to his residence. He saw a person lying by the roadside. Zhang Kai asked him, "Why are you lying here?" The person replied, "I have a foot disease that prevents me from moving. My home is in the south, and I have nowhere to turn for help." Zhang Kai felt pity for him. He had a cart with goods and offered to take him along. Upon reaching his home, the person showed no sign of gratitude and said to Zhang Kai, "Earlier, I was not sick; I was just testing you!" Zhang Kai became very angry and said, "Who are you, and why dare you play tricks on me?" The person replied, "I am a ghost! I was sent by the North Platform Envoy to collect you. Seeing that you are a noble person, I did not want to take you, so I pretended to be sick and lay by the roadside. Earlier, I allowed you to take me in your cart, and I truly appreciate that. However, I was commanded to come, and I am not free to act on my own, what can I do?" Zhang Kai was shocked and asked the ghost to stay, offering a pig and wine as a sacrifice. The ghost accepted the offering and wept, firmly asking for help. The ghost said, "Is there anyone with the same name as you?" Zhang Kai replied, "There is a guest named Huang Kai." The ghost said, "You can go to him, and I will come myself." Zhang Kai went to Huang Kai's house, and the owner came out to see him. The ghost marked his head with a red brand and then pierced his heart with a small knife. The owner felt the pain and the ghost left. The ghost told Zhang Kai, "You have a noble appearance, and I feel sorry for you. I will break the law to help you, but the ways of the gods are mysterious and cannot be revealed." After Zhang Kai left, the owner suddenly felt severe chest pain and died in the middle of the night. Zhang Kai lived to be sixty years old and reached the position of Grand Councillor of the Imperial Household.

    Xie Yun of Liyang, courtesy name Dao Tong, was fifteen years old when he was seized by Wang Mian, a subordinate of the rebel army of Su Jun, and enslaved in the household of Jiang Feng of Dongyang. While traveling through the mountains, he saw a dog trapped in a tiger's cage. Thinking to himself that the dog was hungry, he fed it some rice. When he entered the cage, he saw the tiger and climbed a tree to look down. Xie Yun said to the tiger, "This cage was originally set for you, yet I nearly died in it. You did not kill me, so I will release you." He then opened the cage and let the tiger free. After the rebels were defeated, Xie Yun went to the county office to distinguish the virtuous from the guilty, but Magistrate Zhang Qiu of Wu Cheng did not clear his name, and Xie Yun was imprisoned and subjected to harsh interrogation and punishment. According to the Yuanhe Guangji, he endured all forms of torture. In a dream, Xie Yun heard someone say, "This place is easy to enter but difficult to leave. Because you have a compassionate heart, I will rescue you." When he awoke, he saw a young man dressed entirely in yellow clothing, standing at a distance outside the prison fence. This figure occasionally entered the prison and spoke with Xie Yun. The jailer, realizing this was a supernatural being, thereafter dared not wrongfully accuse Xie Yun.

    A commoner from Xuzhou named Wu Qing was conscripted for military service in the fifth year of the Taiyuan era. The people sacrificed a chicken to pray for good fortune, cooked the chicken head in a tray, and it suddenly made a long sound. Guangji mentions that after this, the enemy was defeated, but the commander Shao Bao died in battle, and the corpses were scattered everywhere, no one could recognize them. Wu Qing saw a person wearing a white brocade robe, suspected it was the commander, and took it to report. Upon examination, it was found to be Shao Bao's head. Wu Qing was promoted to the position of governor of Qinghe due to his merit. From the ranks of soldiers, he suddenly rose to a high position, and the omen of the chicken turned into a good sign.     6

    Zhang Mu from Wu County, residing in Yang Village, East Township, had the character "Jun" added to his name according to the Taiping Yulan. During the reign of Emperor An, a ghost suddenly appeared to help with household tasks. Among Zhang Mu's old household items, there was a broken pot that was no longer useful. The ghost repaired it by drilling the bottom of a jar to make a new pot; by the time the family woke up, the rice was cooked. This ghost had no other needs, only ate sugarcane, and called himself "Gao He." The owner then called him "Ah He." The Taiping Yulan quotes this sentence or says: "This ghost is a kind of vessel," and "Gao He" is a type of gourd. The hills and mounds were piled up, especially with many ancient tombs, and it is suspected that this ghost came from these tombs. Zhang Mu often saw the ghost alone, and it looked like a young girl, as recorded in the Taiping Yulan by Zhang Mu's mother, who saw a young girl about seventeen or eighteen years old, with a blue-black face, wearing a blue robe all over. Then he told Zhang Mu's family to get a white jar and fill it halfway with water, cover the top with a piece of silk, and the next morning when he looked inside, there was something inside, as recorded in the Taiping Yulan as "the jar was full of gold." Zhang Mu's family was originally poor, but they became rich because of this. Once, the ghost said, "Don't hate me, when the days are over, I will leave." As expected, he left. Recorded in the Taiping Guangji, volume 322, and the Taiping Yulan, volumes 947 and 758, briefly.

    7

    A man from Pei County named Qin Shu (also written as "Fū" in Taiping Yulan), lived in Xiaoxin Village, Qu'a. During the reign of Emperor Gong in the Eastern Jin Dynasty (405 CE), he traveled from the capital and, about twenty miles away, lost his way in the dark. Seeing a distant light, he approached it and found a woman holding a candle who said, "I am a weak woman living alone and cannot accommodate guests." Qin Shu replied, "I wish to continue my journey, but it is too dark to proceed, so I humbly ask to stay outside." The woman agreed. After Qin Shu entered and sat down, he noticed that the woman was alone in the room and feared her husband might return, so he could not sleep peacefully. The woman reassured him, saying, "Why should you be so concerned? There is no need to worry, and I will not lead you astray." She prepared food for him, but it was all stale and old. Qin Shu said, "I have not yet married, and I am also unmarried. I wish to form a bond, would you consider it?" The woman smiled and said, "I consider myself unworthy, how could I be a suitable match?" They then spent the night together. At dawn, Qin Shu left, and they both got up to part ways. The woman wept and said, "This is our one meeting, and there will be no more to come." She gave him a pair of finger rings, tied them to his belt, and saw him off at the door. Qin Shu hurriedly left, and after walking several steps, he looked back at where he had stayed and found it to be a tomb. Several days later, he lost the finger rings, but the knot in his belt remained unchanged.

    A Man from Le'an

    Chang Shen of Le'an died of illness, but he revived before being buried. He said: "I was recorded in the celestial bureaucracy, and the judge was my maternal uncle. He judged and released me. I saw a woman who was also recorded, so I removed two pairs of gold bracelets and entrusted them to me to give to the judge, and she was also released. We then feasted together." The woman said: "My home is in Wu, my surname is Xu, and my given name is Qiu Ying." Shen later inquired and finally found her. Her parents then married their daughter to Shen. Imperial Review, 718.

    A Man from Wu-king

    Zhang An of Wu-king fell ill and, just as he was about to recover, he felt something on his bed, and his illness worsened. An used his strength to lift the bedding and catch it. The object transformed into a bird, like an owl, and his malaria was immediately cured. Imperial Review, 743.

    A Man from Pei-kuo

    Zhang Baiyuan of Pei-kuo died of illness at the age of ten. He saw more than ten children under a big hill, pushing a large cart that was several zhang tall. Baiyuan also pushed it. At that time, a violent storm suddenly arose, raising dust. Baiyuan stopped at a mulberry branch and heard a shout. He then returned and revived, with sand and dust in his hair. Later, when he grew older, he reached Mount Tai and recognized the mulberry, as he had seen it when he died. Imperial Review, 955.

    A Man from Pengcheng

    Liu Shamen lived in Pengcheng and died of illness. His wife was poor, and their child was young. They encountered a violent storm, and the walls and buildings were destroyed. His wife cried and hugged the young child, saying, "If your father were alive, how could this happen?" That night, she dreamed of Shamen leading dozens of people to arrange the house and buildings, and they were completed the next day. General Records, 276.

He then placed a board before the bed. The official added, "The term is long, extending far into October; if you do not believe me, on the 15th day of the seventh month at noon, look up at the sky, and you will see something."     Huá Yì of Guangling, who was residing temporarily in Jiangling, returned seven years after his death. At first, they only heard his voice but did not see his form. His family persistently asked and finally managed to see him. He said, "I am in a state of exhaustion and cannot bear to see you." When asked about the reason, he explained, "My original lifespan was not long, but it should not have ended yet. Due to the punishment and beating I received in life, which led me astray, and also killing soldiers and slaves, this reduced my lifespan. After being sent on a mission to Changsha, I will return and pass by again." As expected, he returned and instructed his two sons, "I died early, so you must diligently encourage yourselves. Our family line is in decline; is this the behavior of a son?" He also blamed his brother for not imparting teachings, looking very displeased, and said, "Mèng Yú has already been named in the death register; I just have a limited time left."     A Woman with Two Heads and Four Arms

    During the reign of Emperor Ling, a woman from Luoyang was born with two heads and four arms.

    Translation

    Wen Gui, a nobleman from Qiao County, resided in the capital. One year after his death, his spirit appeared at home, riding in a small carriage with dozens of attendants. He claimed to be the Governor of Beihai. The family prepared food, and he ate everything, but after he left, the containers were still full. The family cried, but Wen Gui said, "Don't cry, I will come soon." Sometimes he came once a month, sometimes every 40 to 50 days, and sometimes he stayed for half a day. The attendants he brought were all dressed in red and were short in stature, sitting and resting among the hedges and in the side rooms, but no one knew who they were. When Wen Gui was about to leave, the family would call out to him to get up, and he would playfully ignore them, treating them as ordinary things. Wen Gui missed his several-year-old grandson, so he brought him over. The ghostly attendants took the child and brought him forward, but the child couldn't withstand the ghostly presence and died, never recognizing anyone again. Wen Gui fetched water and sprayed it on the child, and the child woke up. He saw a peach tree in the courtyard and said, "These peaches were planted by me long ago, and the fruit is very delicious." His wife said, "People say that the dead fear peaches, so why don't you?" He replied, "I dislike peach branches in the southeast that are two feet eight inches long and facing the sun, but I might not fear them."     In Henan, there was a man named Yang Chounu. He often visited Zhang'an Lake to pull up reeds. Just before nightfall, he saw a woman whose clothing was not very elaborate but whose appearance was beautiful. She was on a boat carrying water chestnuts and approached Yang Chounu. She lived by the lake and was forced to stay and unable to return. He then stopped his boat, stayed temporarily, and borrowed utensils to eat. In the dish, there were dried fish and raw vegetables. After eating, they laughed and joked, and Yang Chounu sang and mocked her. The woman replied, "I live by West Lake, and the sun is setting. Sheltered, I met a good host, and unconsciously felt at ease." Soon, the fire was extinguished, and they slept together. He noticed a foul smell and also saw that she had very short fingers, so he suspected she was a demon. This thing knew human intentions, quickly went out the door, turned into a mink, and ran directly into the water. (From Guangji 468)
