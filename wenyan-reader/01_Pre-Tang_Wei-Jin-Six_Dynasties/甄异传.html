    ○ In the second year of Jianwu, Zhang Kai returned from the wilderness to his residence. He saw a person lying by the roadside. Zhang Kai asked him, "Why are you lying here?" The person replied, "I have a foot disease that prevents me from moving. My home is in the south, and I have nowhere to turn for help." Zhang Kai felt pity for him. He had a cart with goods and offered to take him along. Upon reaching his home, the person showed no sign of gratitude and said to Zhang Kai, "Earlier, I was not sick; I was just testing you!" Zhang Kai became very angry and said, "Who are you, and why dare you play tricks on me?" The person replied, "I am a ghost! I was sent by the North Platform Envoy to collect you. Seeing that you are a noble person, I did not want to take you, so I pretended to be sick and lay by the roadside. Earlier, I allowed you to take me in your cart, and I truly appreciate that. However, I was commanded to come, and I am not free to act on my own, what can I do?" Zhang Kai was shocked and asked the ghost to stay, offering a pig and wine as a sacrifice. The ghost accepted the offering and wept, firmly asking for help. The ghost said, "Is there anyone with the same name as you?" Zhang Kai replied, "There is a guest named Huang Kai." The ghost said, "You can go to him, and I will come myself." Zhang Kai went to Huang Kai's house, and the owner came out to see him. The ghost marked his head with a red brand and then pierced his heart with a small knife. The owner felt the pain and the ghost left. The ghost told Zhang Kai, "You have a noble appearance, and I feel sorry for you. I will break the law to help you, but the ways of the gods are mysterious and cannot be revealed." After Zhang Kai left, the owner suddenly felt severe chest pain and died in the middle of the night. Zhang Kai lived to be sixty years old and reached the position of Grand Councillor of the Imperial Household. [2]

He often walked in the mountains and saw a dog in a cage; he secretly thought the dog was hungry and fed it with food. Upon entering the cage, he saw a tiger and climbed a tree to look up. Xie Yun told the tiger, "This cage was meant for you, but I almost died in it. You didn't kill me; I will let you go." He then opened the cage and released the tiger. After the rebellion was suppressed, Xie Yun went to the county office to distinguish between good and bad people. The magistrate of Wucheng, Zhang Qiu, did not help him and instead put him in chains and subjected him to torture. The broad record mentions that he was subjected to all kinds of interrogation. Xie Yun dreamed of someone who said, "It is easy to enter but hard to leave this place. Your kindness will save you." When he woke up, he saw a young man dressed in yellow, standing far outside the prison fence, occasionally entering the prison to speak with him. The prison officials recognized him as a special person and thus did not wrong him.

Grand Commandant Yu Gongliang, upon hearing this, felt compassion and provided him with provisions, so he then arrived in Xiangyang. He followed the priest to Mount Wudang, fasted and abstained for three days, and then met the master, who was the person he had dreamed of before. The master asked him, "Do you wish to see the Yellow Robed Child?" He gave him three pills of divine medicine. Upon taking them, he no longer felt hungry or thirsty, and had no desires or thoughts. The master also has no fixed place. At times, auspicious clouds and purple vapor shroud him, and the fragrant and pleasant smell sometimes permeates the valleys.     A commoner from Xuzhou named Wu Qing was conscripted for military service in the fifth year of the Taiyuan era. The people sacrificed a chicken to pray for good fortune, cooked the chicken head in a tray, and it suddenly made a long sound. Guangji mentions that after this, the enemy was defeated, but the commander Shao Bao died in battle, and the corpses were scattered everywhere, no one could recognize them. Wu Qing saw a person wearing a white brocade robe, suspected it was the commander, and took it to report. Upon examination, it was found to be Shao Bao's head. Wu Qing was promoted to the position of governor of Qinghe due to his merit. From the ranks of soldiers, he suddenly rose to a high position, and the omen of the chicken turned into a good sign.     The concubine Bi Yu of Jinwu Sima Yi was skilled in playing musical instruments and singing. When severely ill, he told Bi Yu: If I die, you should not remarry; if you remarry, I will kill you.     Idiomatic Expressions

    • 忽有鬼来助驱使 - Common idiom in Chinese folklore meaning 'suddenly a ghost came to help with tasks.'
    • 破甑已无所用 - Means 'the broken pot is useless,' indicating something no longer functional.
    • 鬼使撞瓮底穿为甑 - Means 'the ghost made a new pot by drilling the bottom of a jar,' describing a supernatural action.
    • 比家人起，饭已熟 - Means 'by the time the family woke up, the rice was cooked,' indicating the ghost's efficiency.
    • 此鬼无他须，唯啖甘蔗 - Means 'this ghost had no other needs, only ate sugarcane,' describing the ghost's peculiar habit.
    • 林每独见之 - Means 'Zhang Mu often saw it alone,' indicating the ghost's solitary appearance.
    • 形如少女 - Means 'it looked like a young girl,' describing the ghost's appearance.
    • 面青黑色，遍身青衣 - Means 'with a blue-black face, wearing a blue robe all over,' further detailing the ghost's appearance.

    A Woman with Two Heads and Four Arms

    During the reign of Emperor Ling, a woman from Luoyang was born with two heads and four arms.

    Translation

    Wen Gui, a nobleman from Qiao County, resided in the capital. One year after his death, his spirit appeared at home, riding in a small carriage with dozens of attendants. He claimed to be the Governor of Beihai. The family prepared food, and he ate everything, but after he left, the containers were still full. The family cried, but Wen Gui said, "Don't cry, I will come soon." Sometimes he came once a month, sometimes every 40 to 50 days, and sometimes he stayed for half a day. The attendants he brought were all dressed in red and were short in stature, sitting and resting among the hedges and in the side rooms, but no one knew who they were. When Wen Gui was about to leave, the family would call out to him to get up, and he would playfully ignore them, treating them as ordinary things. Wen Gui missed his several-year-old grandson, so he brought him over. The ghostly attendants took the child and brought him forward, but the child couldn't withstand the ghostly presence and died, never recognizing anyone again. Wen Gui fetched water and sprayed it on the child, and the child woke up. He saw a peach tree in the courtyard and said, "These peaches were planted by me long ago, and the fruit is very delicious." His wife said, "People say that the dead fear peaches, so why don't you?" He replied, "I dislike peach branches in the southeast that are two feet eight inches long and facing the sun, but I might not fear them." The above is a brief excerpt from Classical Collections 86, Imperial Reader 967, Essential Techniques for the Common People 10, and Imperial Reader 967. It is observed that he dislikes garlic but fears peaches. This is recorded in Comprehensive Records 325. [10]
