    Using Mugwort Leaves to Repel Fleas and Lice

    Place mugwort leaves under your mat to repel fleas and lice.

    Taoist Term for Vinegar

    The immortals refer to vinegar as "Hua Chi Zuo Wei."

    Removing White Hair and Preventing Black Hair Growth

    White hair must be removed with tweezers and wax must be applied to prevent black hair from growing in the pores.

    Medicinal Concoction

    Mix equal parts of pine resin, almond, jujube flesh, and poria, and take fifty grains after meals to avoid hunger. Avoid eating pork if you have yellow gentian or platycodon. If you have poria, avoid vinegar. If you have fine ginger, avoid raw vegetables.

    Ancient Mirrors

    Ancient mirrors were not made purely of copper; they all contained tin.

    Toxicity of Croton

    Even immortals would die if they ate one seed of croton. A rat eating it for three years would weigh thirty catties.

    Folk Belief About Metal

    Flour gold is actually snake droppings.

    Superstition About the Zheng Surname

    Those with the Zheng surname should avoid certain actions on the day of the pig (亥) and use fire to ward off bad luck on that day.

    Belief About Killing Snakes

    On the day of the snake (巳) or in the year of the snake, one should not kill snakes.

    Taoist Deities

    According to Duan Chengshi, the deities Shentu and Yulu lead ten thousand spirits.

    Malaria and Cultural Perceptions

    The malaria spirit is small and cannot harm giants, hence the saying that heroes do not suffer from malaria. The people of Jin say that gentlemen do not suffer from malaria. The people of Shu consider intermittent malaria to be a form of servant malaria.

    Classification of Pearls

    According to Shen Huaiyuan's Nan Yue Zhi, pearls are classified into nine grades. Those measuring more than five-tenths of an inch up to eight-tenths of an inch are the highest grade. They have a lustrous appearance and one side is slightly flat, resembling a golden cover. These are called dangzhu pearls. Below dangzhu pearls are zouzhu pearls, followed by hua pearls, leiguo pearls, guanyu pearls, shui pearls, and cong pearls.

    Rare Animal and Wine Making

    In the second year of the Yuanfeng era (109 BCE), the Roman Empire presented a cow with spotted hooves, six feet tall, with its tail wrapped around its horns, and four ears. The insect known as the "Asa Department Wax Deer" was used to make wax. Its meat was cut and layered with stones to press out the juice, which was then mixed with herbs from Persia and other regions and fermented for several days to become wine. Drinking this wine can intoxicate one.
