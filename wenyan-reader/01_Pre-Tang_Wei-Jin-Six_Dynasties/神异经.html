    神异经　　汉 东方朔

    　　东方荒外有豫章焉（樟木），树主九州。其高千丈，围百丈，本上三百丈，本如有条枝，敷张如帐，上有玄狐黑猿。枝主一州，南北并列，面向西南。有九力士操斧伐之，以占九州吉凶。斫复，其州有福；迟者州伯有病；积岁不复者，其州灭亡（亡言州伯死，复者木创复也）。

    　　东方有桑树焉，高八十丈，敷张自辅。其叶长一丈，广六七尺。其上自有蚕，作茧长三尺，缲一茧，得丝一斤。有椹焉，长三尺五寸，围如长（此桑是间桑，但树长大）。

    The Book of Strange and Marvelous Things　　Han Dynasty, Dongfang Shuo

    Beyond the eastern wilderness, there is a magnolia tree, which governs the nine regions. The tree is a thousand feet tall and a hundred feet in circumference, with its trunk rising three hundred feet. Its branches and leaves spread out like a tent, and there are black foxes and black apes on it. Each branch governs one region, arranged north and south, facing southwest. There are nine strong men wielding axes to cut it, to predict the good or bad fortune of the nine regions. If it is cut and regrows, the region will have good fortune; if it grows slowly, the governor of the region will be ill; if it does not regrow after many years, the region will perish (the phrase "亡言州伯死" means "the governor of the region will die," and "复者木创复也" means "regrowing is the tree healing itself").

    In the east, there is a mulberry tree, eighty feet high and spreading out to support itself. Its leaves are a foot long and six or seven feet wide. The tree has its own silkworms, which produce cocoons three feet long, and one cocoon produces one pound of silk. There are mulberries, three feet and five inches long, with a circumference as long as their length (this mulberry is a special kind, but the tree is very large).

    荒外有火山，其中生不昼之木，昼夜火燃，得暴风不猛，猛雨不灭。

    　　南方大荒之中有树焉，名曰祖、稼、■〈木匿〉。祖，柤梨也；稼，株稼也；■〈木匿〉，亲■〈木匿〉也。三千岁作花，九千岁作实。其花蕊紫色，其实赤色，其高百丈，或千丈也。敷张自辅，东西南北方。枝各近五十丈，叶长七尺，广四尺，色如绿青，禾皮如桂，树理如甘草，味饴，实长九尺，围如长，无瓤核，竹刀割之如酥，得食复见实即灭矣（张茂先注曰：言复见后实熟者，寿一万二千岁）。

    　　南方大荒有树焉，名曰如何。三百岁作花，九百岁作实。花色朱，其实正黄。高五十丈，敷张如盖，叶长一丈，广二尺馀，似菅苎，色青，厚五分，可以絮如厚朴材理如支九子，味如饴，实有核，形如棘子。长五尺，围如长，金刀剖之则酸，芦刀剖之则辛。食之者地仙，不畏水火，不畏白刃（刃刀之属。言地仙者，不能飞在地，从人去也）。

    In the outer wilderness, there is a volcano, within which grows a tree that never sleeps, burning day and night. This tree does not diminish in the face of strong winds, nor does it extinguish in heavy rain.

    In the southern wilderness, there is a tree known as Zu, Jia, and ■〈木匿〉. Zu is a pear tree; Jia is a fruit-bearing tree; ■〈木匿〉 is a specific type of tree. After three thousand years, it blooms; after nine thousand years, it bears fruit. Its flower buds are purple, and its fruit is red. It stands a hundred or even a thousand feet tall. It spreads out and supports itself, extending in all directions. Each branch is nearly fifty feet long, with leaves that are seven feet long and four feet wide, colored like greenish-blue. The bark is like that of cinnamon, and the wood is like licorice, with a sweet taste. The fruit is nine feet long, as wide as it is long, and contains no pulp or seeds. When cut with a bamboo knife, it is as smooth as butter, and if eaten, it will disappear immediately (as noted by Zhang Maoxian: "If one sees the fruit ripen again, they will live for twelve thousand years").

    In the southern wilderness, there is another tree named "How." After three hundred years, it blooms; after nine hundred years, it bears fruit. Its flowers are red, and its fruit is bright yellow. It stands fifty feet tall, spreading out like an umbrella. Its leaves are ten feet long and over two feet wide, resembling reeds or hemp, with a green color and a thickness of five fen. It can be used like thick bark, with a texture like that of nine children, and a sweet taste. The fruit has a core, shaped like a thorn. It is five feet long and as wide as it is long. When cut with a golden knife, it is sour; when cut with a reed knife, it is spicy. Those who eat it become earth immortals, not fearing water and fire, nor fearing sharp blades (referring to earth immortals who cannot fly but stay on the ground, accompanying humans).

    东方有树焉，高百丈，敷张自辅。叶长一丈，广六七尺，名梨，如今之柤梨，但树大耳。其子径三尺，剖之少瓤，白如素，和羹食之，为地仙。衣服不败，辟穀，可以入水火也。

    　　东南荒中有邪木焉，高三千丈，或十馀围，或七八尺。其枝乔直上不可■〈冉阝〉也。叶如甘瓜，二百岁叶落而生花，花形如甘瓜，复二百岁落尽而生萼，萼下生子，三岁而成熟。成熟之后，不长不减，子形如寒瓜（似冬瓜也），长七八寸，径四五寸。萼复覆生顶（言发萼而得成实），此不取，万世如故。若取子而留萼，萼复生子如初，年月复成熟，复二年则成萼，而复生子。其子形如甘瓤，少■〈柬见〉，甘美，食之令人身泽。不可过三升，令人冥醉，半曰乃醒。木高，人取不能得，唯木下有多罗之人，缘能得之（多罗国名）。一名无叶，世人后生不见叶，故谓之无叶也。一名倚骄（张茂先注曰：骄直上不可那也）。

    3

    In the east, there is a tree that stands a hundred zhang tall, spreading and supporting itself. Its leaves are one zhang long and six or seven chi wide. It is called the plum tree, similar to modern plums, but much larger. Its fruit is three chi in diameter, has little flesh, and is white like silk. Eating it with soup makes one a local immortal. It preserves clothing, allows one to abstain from food, and enables one to enter water and fire.

    In the southeast wilderness, there is a strange tree that is 3,000 zhang tall, with a circumference of over ten zhang or seven or eight chi. Its branches are tall and straight, making it impossible to climb. Its leaves are like melons, and after 200 years, the leaves fall and flowers bloom. The flowers are like melons, and after another 200 years, the flowers fall and calyces grow. The calyces produce fruit, which matures in three years. After maturation, the fruit does not grow or shrink and is shaped like a winter melon, about seven or eight cun long and four or five cun in diameter. The calyces continue to grow at the top, forming the fruit. If not harvested, the tree will remain unchanged for eternity. If the fruit is harvested but the calyces are left, they will regrow the fruit in the same manner, repeating the cycle. The fruit is like a sweet melon, slightly bitter, and eating it makes one's skin smooth. One should not eat more than three sheng, as it can cause deep intoxication, taking half a day to sober up. The tree is too high for people to reach, but those from the Dora people can climb it. Another name is "leafless," as later generations do not see the leaves, hence the name. Another name is "arrogant," meaning the tree is so tall and straight that it cannot be reached.

    东方有树，高五十丈，叶长八尺，名曰桃。其子径三尺二寸，小核味和，和核羹食之，令人益寿。食核中仁，可以治嗽。小桃温润，既嗽，人食之即止。

    　　北方荒中有枣林焉，其高五十丈，敷张枝条数里馀，疾风不能偃，雷电不能摧。其子长六七寸，围过其长，熟赤如朱，乾之不缩，气味润泽，殊於常枣，食之可以安躯，益於气力。此枣枝条盛於常枣，亦益气安躯。赤松子云，北方大枣，味有殊，既可益气，又安躯。

    　　南方荒中有涕竹，长数百丈，围三丈五六尺，厚八九寸，可以为船。其笋甘美，煮食之可以止创疠（张茂先注曰：子笋也）。

    　　南方山有邯■〈甘庶〉之林，其高百丈，围三尺八寸。促节多汁，甜如蜜，咋啮其汁，令人润泽，可以节蚘虫。人腹中蚘虫，其状如蚓，此消穀虫也，多则伤人，少则穀不消，是甘蔗能减多益少，凡蔗亦然。

    4

    In the east, there is a tree fifty zhang tall, with leaves eight chi long, named peach. Its fruit has a diameter of three chi two cun, and the small pit has a harmonious taste. When eaten in a soup, it can make one live longer. Eating the kernel inside the pit can cure cough. The small peach is warm and moist, and when someone has a cough, eating it can stop the cough.

    In the northern wilderness, there is a date forest fifty zhang tall, with branches spread out for several li. Strong winds cannot bend it, and lightning cannot destroy it. The fruits are six or seven cun long, with a circumference greater than their length. When ripe, they are red like cinnabar, and when dried, they do not shrink. Their flavor and aroma are moist and lustrous, different from ordinary dates. Eating them can strengthen the body and enhance physical strength. This date tree has more abundant branches than ordinary date trees and also strengthens the body and enhances physical strength. The Red Pine Sage says that northern dates have a unique flavor and can both enhance physical strength and strengthen the body.

    In the southern wilderness, there is a bamboo called "tis" hundreds of zhang long, with a circumference of three zhang five or six chi, and a thickness of eight or nine cun. It can be used to make boats. The bamboo shoots are sweet and delicious, and when cooked and eaten, they can stop sores and leprosy.

    In the southern mountains, there is a grove of sugarcane one hundred zhang tall, with a circumference of three chi eight cun. The joints are short and the cane is juicy, sweet like honey. Chewing the juice makes one's skin moist and lustrous, and it can control worms. Worms in the human body are like earthworms, and these are grain-eating worms. Too many worms can harm a person, and too few can prevent grains from being digested. Sugarcane can reduce the number of worms and increase their beneficial effects. All sugarcane is the same.

    不昼木火中有鼠，重千斤，毛长二尺余，细如丝。但居火中洞赤，时时出外。而毛白，以水逐而沃之即死。取其毛绩纺织以为布，用之若有垢涴，以火烧之则净也。

    　　北方层冰万里，厚百丈。有磎鼠在水下土中焉，形如鼠，食草木，肉重千斤，可以作脯，食之已热。其毛八尺，可以为褥，卧之却寒。其皮可以蒙鼓，闻千里。其毛可以来鼠，此尾所在鼠聚。今江南鼠，食草木为灾，此类也。

    　　西方山中有蛇，头尾差大，有色五彩。人物触之者，中头则尾至，中尾则头至，中腰则头尾并至，名曰率然（张茂先注曰：会稽常山最多此蛇。故孙子兵法“三军势如率然者”是也）。

    　　北海有大鸟，其高千尺，头文曰天，胸文曰侯，左翼丈曰鷖，右翼文曰勒。头向东正，海中央捕鱼。或时举翼而飞，其羽相切如风雷也。

    In the firewood that is not diurnal, there is a rat that weighs a thousand catties, with hair growing more than two feet long, fine as silk. It resides in the fire, its body deeply red, and often comes out. Its hair is white, and if chased with water and doused, it dies. Its hair can be spun and woven into cloth, which, if used, becomes dirty, but burning it with fire makes it clean.

    In the north, layers of ice stretch for ten thousand miles, thick for a hundred feet. There are rats in the water and soil beneath, shaped like rats, feeding on grass and trees, with flesh weighing a thousand catties, which can be made into dried meat, eating which cures heat. Its hair is eight feet long, which can be made into a cushion, lying on which wards off cold. Its skin can be used to cover drums, heard for a thousand miles. Its hair can attract rats, and where its tail is, rats gather. Today, rats in the south of the Yangtze River, feeding on grass and trees, cause disasters, such as these.

    In the mountains of the west, there are snakes with heads and tails slightly larger, with five colors. People and things that touch it: if it touches the head, the tail arrives; if it touches the tail, the head arrives; if it touches the waist, both head and tail arrive. It is called Shuoran (Zhang Maoxian commented: the most of this snake are in Kuaiji and Changshan. So the Sunzi Art of War says, "the three armies are like Shuoran").

    In the north sea, there is a large bird, its height a thousand feet, its head marked with the character Tian, its chest marked with the character Hou, its left wing marked with the character Yi, its right wing marked with the character Le. Its head faces east, straight, fishing in the middle of the sea. Sometimes it raises its wings and flies, its feathers brushing together like wind and thunder.

    北方荒中有石湖，方千里，岸深五丈余，恒冰，惟夏至左右五六十曰解耳。有横公鱼，长七八尺，形如鲤而目赤，昼在湖中，夜化为人，刺之不入，煮之不死。以乌梅二枚煮之则熟，食之可止邪病。其湖无凸凹，平满无高下。

    　　南方蚊翼下有蜚虫焉，目明者见之。每生九卵。复未常有孵复成九子。蜚而俱去，蚊遂不知。亦食人及百兽。食者知言虫小食人不去也。此虫既细且小，因曰细蠛，陈章对齐桓公小虫是也。此虫常春生，以季夏藏于鹿耳中，名婴蜺，亦细小也。

    　　八荒之中有毛人焉，长七八尺，皆如人形。身及头上，皆有毛，如猕猴。毛长尺馀，■〈生毛〉■〈丽毛〉，见人则瞑目，开口吐舌，上唇覆面，下唇覆胸。喜食人。舌鼻牵引共戏，不与即去，名曰髯公，俗曰髯丽。一名髯狎小儿，髯狎，可畏也（字未详）。

    6

    In the remote northern wilderness, there is a stone lake, measuring a thousand li in extent, with shores deep by more than five zhang, perpetually frozen. It thaws only for about fifty to sixty days around the summer solstice. There is a fish known as the Henggong fish, which grows to a length of seven or eight chi, resembling a carp but with red eyes. By day, it resides in the lake, but by night, it transforms into a human. It cannot be pierced by a spear and cannot be killed by boiling. Cooking it with two pieces of plum makes it edible, and eating it can cure evil diseases. The lake is flat and level, without any elevation or depression.

    Under the wings of mosquitoes in the south, there are tiny insects that can only be seen by those with keen eyesight. Each insect lays nine eggs, and when they hatch, they produce nine offspring. The insects fly away together, and the mosquitoes remain unaware. These insects also feed on humans and animals. Those who eat them know that despite their small size, they do not leave the body. These insects are both small and minute, hence named "Ximai." They are the same small insects mentioned by Chen Zhang to Duke Huan of Qi. These insects are born in spring and hide in deer ears during late summer, named "Yingni," which are also small and minute.

    In the remote and desolate regions of the eight directions, there are hairy people, about seven to eight feet tall, all resembling humans in shape. Their entire body and head are covered with hair, like that of a macaque. Their hair is about a foot long, lush and beautiful. When they see people, they close their eyes, stick out their tongues, and their upper lip covers their face while their lower lip covers their chest. They enjoy eating humans. They playfully pull people's tongues and noses; if they are not allowed to play, they will leave. They are called "Rangan Gong" or "Ranli," and another name for them is "Ranxia Xiao'er." "Ranxia" suggests that they are to be feared due to their playful and potentially harmful nature.

    西海之外有鹄国焉，男女皆长七寸，为人自然有礼，好经编跪拜。其人皆寿三百岁。行如飞，曰行千里，百物不敢犯之。惟畏海鹄，过辄吞之，亦寿三百岁。此人在鹄腹中不死，而鹄亦一举千里（张茂先注曰：陈章与齐桓公论小儿是也）。

    　　西北荒中有玉馈之酒，酒泉注焉，广一丈长，深三丈，酒美如肉，澄清如镜，上有玉尊玉笾。取一尊，一尊复生焉。与天同休，无乾时。石边有脯焉，味如獐鹿脯，饮此酒，人不生死。此（阙）间人与天生同，虽男不（阙）妇，故言不生死。

    　　西荒中有人焉，面目手足皆人形，而胁下有翼，不能飞，名曰苗民。《书》曰：窜三苗于三危，西裔，为人饕餮淫泆无礼，故窜於此。

    Beyond the Western Sea lies the land of the Swans, where the men and women are all seven inches tall. They are naturally polite and enjoy performing rituals such as kneeling and bowing. The people there all live for three hundred years. They can fly like birds, covering a thousand miles in a single leap, and nothing dares to harm them. However, they fear the sea swans, which, when they pass by, will swallow them whole, also granting them a lifespan of three hundred years. These people remain unharmed within the swan's belly, and the swan can also fly a thousand miles in one leap (as Zhang Maoxian noted: "Chen Zhang and Duke Huan of Qi discussed the matter of the child").

    In the northwest wasteland, there is the Jade Wine, flowing from a spring of wine. It is one zhang wide and three zhang deep, with wine that tastes like meat and is as clear as a mirror. Above it are jade cups and plates. When one cup is taken, another immediately replenishes. It lasts as long as heaven and never runs out. Beside the stone, there is a kind of dried meat that tastes like deer meat. Drinking this wine, one does not experience death or life. The people in this (missing) land are the same as heaven, even though they are not (missing) women, so it is said that they do not experience death or life.

    In the far west, there are people who have human faces and hands but have wings on their sides, unable to fly. They are called the Miao people. The Book of Documents says: "The Miao were exiled to the Three Dangers in the western frontier, where they were gluttonous, licentious, and disrespectful, hence they were exiled there."

    西北荒中有小人，长一分。其君朱衣玄冠，乘辂车马，引为威仪。居人遇其乘车，抓而食之，其味辛，终年不为（阙）所咋并识万物名字。又杀腹中三虫，三虫死，便可食仙药也（一分字恐有误）。

    　　南有人焉，周行天下。其长七丈，腹围如其长，朱衣缟带，以赤蛇绕其项，不饮不食，朝吞恶鬼三千，暮吞三百。此人以鬼为饭，以露为浆，名曰尺郭，一名黄父。

    　　西方曰宫之外有山焉，其长十余里，广二三里，高百余丈，皆大黄之金。其色殊美，不杂土石，不生草木，上有金人，高五丈余，皆纯金，名曰金犀。入山下一丈有银，又入一丈有锡，又入一丈有铅，又入一丈有丹阳铜，似金可锻，以作错涂之器也（张茂先注曰：淮南子术曰“饵丹阳之为金”是也）。

    　　西荒之中有人焉，长短如人，著败衣，手虎爪，名獏■〈犭为〉。伺人独行，辄食人脑。或舌出盘地丈馀，人先闻声，烧大石以投其舌，乃气绝而死，不然食人脑矣。

    8

    In the remote northwest, there are small people, each a mere inch in height. Their ruler wears a red robe and black cap, rides in a grand carriage, and serves as a symbol of dignity and respect. When residents encounter them riding in their carriages, they grab and eat them, and the taste is pungent. Throughout the year, they are not affected by (something) and know the names of all things. They also kill three worms inside their bodies, and upon the worms' death, they can eat elixirs of immortality (the character "分" may be in error).

    In the south, there is someone who travels throughout the world. This person is seven zhang tall, with a waist circumference equal to their height, wears a red robe and white belt, and has a red snake coiling around their neck. They do not drink or eat, swallowing 3,000 evil spirits in the morning and 300 in the evening. This person uses ghosts as food and dew as drink, and is known as Chige, also called Huangfu.

    Outside the palace in the west, there is a mountain, stretching over ten li in length, two or three li in width, and more than a hundred zhang in height. It is filled with a type of gold called "dahuang," which has exceptionally beautiful colors, is not mixed with earth or stones, and does not grow plants. On top of the mountain, there are golden figures, each over five zhang tall, all made of pure gold, known as "jinxi." At one zhang below the mountain, there is silver; another zhang down, tin; another zhang down, lead; and another zhang down, "danyang" copper, which looks like gold and can be forged, used to make polished and decorated objects (as noted by Zhang Maoxian: the Huainanzi technique states that "the essence of danyang is gold").

    In the remote west, there are people whose height is like that of ordinary humans, wearing tattered clothes and having tiger claws. They wait for people to walk alone and immediately eat their brains. Sometimes their tongue extends over a distance of more than one zhang, and people hear the sound first. By throwing a heated stone at the tongue, they can be killed from lack of breath; otherwise, they would eat human brains.

    南荒之外有火山，昼夜火燃。火中有鼠重百斤，毛长二尺余，细如丝，可以作布。恒居火中，时时出外而白，以水逐而沃之乃死，取其毛缉织以为布。

    　　南方之兽如鹿豕头，善依人求五穀，名无损兽。人割取肉不病，肉自复，其肉惟可作鮓，使■〈米审〉肥美，而咋肉不坏，吞之不入。■〈米审〉尽更澡肉，复作鮓如初，愈美，名不尽鮓是也。
