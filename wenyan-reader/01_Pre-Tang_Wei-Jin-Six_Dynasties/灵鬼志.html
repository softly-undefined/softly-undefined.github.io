Later, after the emperor was moved to Shitou, the imperial food supplies were insufficient. (This is quoted in the commentary of the Shi Shuo Xin Yu on the section "Rong Zhi")

    When Yu Wenkang first took control of Wuchang, he left Shitou, and the people on the shore sang a song: "Yu Gong goes to Wuchang, gracefully like a flying bird; Yu Gong returns to Yangzhou, riding a white horse with ceremonial flags." Another version says: "When Yu Gong first went up, he was graceful like a flying bird; when Yu Gong returned to Yangzhou, he rode a white horse with a ceremonial carriage." Afterward, despite repeated calls to return, he did not go back, and soon after, he died in the capital and was buried there. (This is quoted in the commentary of the Shi Shuo Xin Yu on the section "Shang Shi")

    Initially, when Huan Shimin was the governor of Jingzhou and stationed in Shangming, the people suddenly sang a song about Huang Tan: "Huang Tan Ying, the Great Buddha of Yangzhou comes to Shangming." Soon after, Huan Shimin died, and Wang Chen became the governor of Jingzhou. "Foda" was Wang Chen's nickname. (This is quoted in the commentary of the Shi Shuo Xin Yu on the section "Tai Chi")

    After Hejian Wang Yong suffered defeat in Guanzhong, there was a servant named Chen An who was very strong and healthy: he often rode a red horse, which was exceptionally swift; he carried two swords, both seven feet long; when he rode his horse and wielded his swords, he was invincible wherever he went. The people of Guanxi sang a song in his honor: "The strong warrior Chen An on the ridge, with a slender head, narrow face, and broad chest, wielding an eight-zhang spear that spins left and right." Zhang Ying, being skilled, woke up and used fire to make high seats and a seat for the goddess of children. The next morning, at mealtime, Master Zhi Fajing arrived, and the high seats were ready. After hearing about the dream, the couple took the Five Precepts. The wife's illness soon improved.
