    Idiomatic Expressions

    • 松脂沦入地中，千岁为茯苓，伏神 - Describes the transformation of pine resin into *fu ling* (poria cocos) and *fu shen* (a type of fungus) after a thousand years.
    • 枫脂沦入地中，千秋为虎珀 - Describes the transformation of maple resin into *hu bo* (amber) after a thousand years.
    • 削玉如铁刀削木 - Describes the sharpness of a diamond knife, capable of cutting jade like an iron knife cuts wood.
    • 大者长尺许，小者如稻米 - Describes the size range of diamonds, from large to small.
    • 欲刻玉时，当作大金镮，着手指间，开其背如月，以割玉刀内镮中，以刻玉 - Describes the process of using a diamond knife to carve jade, involving a large gold ring.
    • 五肉七菜，胜腌腥臊 - Describes a dietary preference for five meats and seven vegetables over preserved and strong-smelling foods.

    Cultural and Historical Allusions

    • 大秦国 - Refers to the Roman Empire, often used in ancient Chinese texts to describe the Western world.
    • 西海 - Refers to the western sea, likely the Mediterranean Sea.
    • 天竺 - Refers to India, a common term in ancient Chinese texts.
    • 大月氐 - Refers to a historical region, possibly the Kushan Empire.
    • 五色颇黎 - Refers to colored glass, often used in jewelry and decorative items.
    • 木难 - Refers to a type of gemstone, possibly a precious stone or mineral.

    Maple resin, when seeped into the earth, becomes hu bo (amber) after a thousand years. Yu luan 808, you yang za zu 11, citing that maple resin becomes amber when it enters the earth.

    Coral originates from the western sea of Daqin (Roman Empire), growing on stones underwater. It is initially white, turns yellow after one year, red after three years, and is consumed by insects and decays after four years. Yu luan 807.

    Diamond comes from Tianzhu (India) in Daqin (Roman Empire), also known as xue yu dao (diamond knife). It can cut jade as easily as an iron knife cuts wood. Large ones are about a foot long, while small ones are the size of rice grains. When carving jade, one should use a large gold ring, place it on the finger, open the back like a crescent moon, and insert the jade-cutting knife into the ring to carve the jade. Yu luan 813.

    Gold rings come from Tianzhu (India) in Daqin (Roman Empire). Shu chao 136.

    Che qu (a type of shell) comes from Tianzhu (India). Lei ju 84, yu luan 808.

    Daqin (Roman Empire) has five-colored po li (glass); red is the most valuable. Yu luan 808.

    Mù nán (a type of gemstone) comes from Daqin (Roman Empire). Yu luan 809.
