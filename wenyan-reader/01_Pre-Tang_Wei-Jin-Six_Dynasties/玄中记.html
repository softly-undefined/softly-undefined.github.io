    The Record of the Mysterious Center [Xuanzhong Ji] by Guo Pu of the Jin Dynasty

    • Fuxi had a dragon body, and Nüwa had a snake body. In the annotations to the "Records of the Mysterious Center" by Guo Pu, it is mentioned that the three sons of Zhuanxu all died prematurely and dwelled in human palaces, good at frightening children.
    • During the Han dynasty, a ritual was performed using the Five Camps and a Thousand Cavalrymen, carrying torches from the main gate of the imperial palace to send off the plague, and then throwing it into the Luo River. This ritual is recorded in the "Records of Customs and Festivals of Jingchu" and the "Jade Candle Treasury."

    2

    刑天 fought with the emperor as a deity; the emperor cut off his head and buried him on Chángyáng Mountain, then transformed his breasts into eyes and Qí into a mouth. The Shanhaijing and Xuanzhongji also record this story.

    Yin Shou made a mirror. The Yuelu records this in volume 717, and the Hailu Sui Shi in volume 5.

    Xun Shi made a hat. The Tongdian records this in volume 57, and the Tongzhi Lüe records it as a hat.

    The Dog-sealing Clan: Gaoxin had a beautiful woman who was not married. The Xianrong caused chaos, and the emperor said, "Whoever subdues them, I will marry his daughter to him and grant him 300 households." The emperor's dog was named Panhu. In three months, he killed the Xianrong and brought back their heads. The emperor thought it was not suitable to train the people, so he married his daughter to him and sent him to live in exile in the southeast of Kuaiji, 21,000 li away, where he obtained land in the sea, covering an area of 3,000 li. Those born as males became dogs, and those born as females became beautiful women. He was enfeoffed as the ruler of a dog kingdom. The Leiju records this in volume 94, and it is also found in Lushih Fashu as "the emperor married his daughter to him, and it was not suitable to train the people, so he sent him to live in exile." The last sentence reads, "This is the origin of the Dog-sealing Clan."

    The Husbandmen: Emperor Taipu of Yin sent Wang Ying to gather herbs from the Queen Mother of the West. Upon reaching this point, they ran out of food and could not proceed, so they ate the fruit of trees and wore tree bark. He remained unmarried for life, and two children were born from his back, and his father died. This is the origin of the husbandmen. They lived 20,000 li from Yumen. The Yuelu records this in volume 361.

    The People of Fu Fu: A minister of the Yellow Emperor, Xuan Yuan, named Qie Feng, was punished and exiled. He crawled away, and later became known as the People of Fu Fu, living two thousand five hundred li away from the Jade Gate. (Yuelan 797)

    The Transformed People: They ate leaves for three to seven years, transforming themselves to wrap themselves like silkworms spinning silk, growing wings in nine years, and dying in ten years. They lived four thousand li away from Langya. (Yuelan 825)

    The Qiheng Clan: Skilled in strange and ingenious crafts, they could build flying vehicles and travel far with the wind. (Yuelan 752)

    The Land of the Virtuous: An area of a thousand li, abundant with hibiscus flowers. (Leiju 89)

    The Customs of Yi and Tangwu: The customs of Yi are the same as those of Tangwu, with people living in caves, located ten thousand li away from the Jade Gate. (Shuchao 158)

    The People of Flying Paths: Living in cold regions, in caves, eating the roots of trees. (Shuchao 158)

    The People of Ding: Living in cold regions, in caves, eating the meat of birds and mice. They are known as Mingqiu, possibly meaning "famous fur." (Shuchao 158)

    Zhuwu County: The people serve as laborers, living near the coast. When giving birth, they use sand and stones to support themselves. They do not eat rice, relying solely on fish for sustenance. (Yuelan 361)

    To the west of Wu, there is the Lake of Juqu, with Mount Bao inside, which has the Sacred Hall of Dongting. This is also mentioned in Chuxue Ji 8. They enter underground and travel to Langya Dongwu. (Huanyu Ji 91, Yuelan 46)

    4

    Sichuan has Qingcheng Mountain, which has a cave that leads underground, dividing into three paths, each leading to a different place. Alternatively, it can be read as: "Dividing into three holes, leading northwest to Kunlun."

    North of Pengcheng, there is Jiu Li Mountain, with a cave that leads to Langya and also to Wangwu, commonly called the Yellow Pool Cave.

    Water, the most abundant thing in the world, floats in the sky and bears the earth, reaching every high and low place, moistening all things.

    The strongest thing in the world is the Woji of the East Sea: water flows into it without stopping. Woji is the name of a mountain, located 30,000 li south of the East Sea. Sea water flows into it and immediately disappears, therefore, the water flows southeast without overflowing.

    The weakest thing in the world is the weak water of Kunlun: even a goose feather cannot float on it.

    The largest thing in the world is the crab of the North Sea; lifting one claw can make it higher than a mountain, but its body remains in the water.

    5

    The greatest in the southeast is the giant turtle: carrying the Mount Penglai on its back, with a circumference of a thousand miles. The Wenxuan Siyuan Fu annotation in Yulian 38 refers to the giant turtle as a giant turtle. Chuxue Ji 30 and Wu Du Fu annotation in Wenxuan refer to the giant turtle as a giant turtle.

    In the eastern sea, there is a great fish: Guangji refers to it as a great fish in the eastern sea. Those who travel the sea will encounter the fish's head for one day and the fish's tail for seven days. Its spawning will turn three hundred miles into blood. Yulian 936 and Guangji 464 refer to it as producing three days of redness in the green sea.

    The highest in the world is the branchless tree of Fusang: reaching up to the heavens, coiling and bending downward. Shilei Fu annotation 25 refers to it as coiling downward to connect with the three springs. Qimin Yaoshu 10 and Yulian 955.

    The largest of the fruit-bearing trees is the peach of Mount Jishan: as large as a ten-ho container. Qimin Yaoshu 10, Leiju 88, Chuxue Ji 28, and Yulian 967.

    In the east is a place called Chaidu, in the state of Qi. Yulian 1 refers to these three words, and Chaidu is also called Zhu. There is a mountain, with a spring on top, like a well, with an unfathomable depth; in spring and summer, it often produces rain and hail. Yulian refers to it as producing hail from the well in spring and summer, damaging the five grains. People use firewood to block it, then it does not produce; if not blocked with firewood, it will produce. Shuchao 152, Yulian 14, and Guangji 399.

    6

    In the south, there is a volcanic mountain known as the Fire Mountain. According to the Yuyan (Imperial Inspection), the word "fire" appears in the eastern part of Funan, north of Jia Ying, and west of Zhubo. The mountain starts to emit fire in April and extinguishes it in December. In January, February, and March, the fire does not burn; instead, the mountain only emits clouds, and grass and trees grow leaves and branches. By April, the fire burns, and grass and trees lose their leaves, just like in China during cold weather. Travelers pass by the foot of this mountain in January, February, and March, taking firewood or cutting the wood for firewood, which burns without end. They also take the bark and weave it to make fire-washed cloth. This is recorded in the Leiji (Classified Records), volume 80, the Yuyan (Imperial Inspection), volume 868, and the Shileifu Zushi (Annotations on the Categories of Assignments), volume 8.

    In the north, there is a Bell Mountain. At the top of the mountain, there is a stone that looks like a human head. The left eye is the sun, and the right eye is the moon. Opening the left eye signifies daytime, and opening the right eye signifies nighttime. Opening the mouth represents spring and summer, while closing the mouth represents autumn and winter. This is recorded in the Yuyan (Imperial Inspection), volume 38.

    In the southeast, there is a Peach Capital Mountain. At the top of the mountain, there is a large tree named Peach Capital, whose branches are three thousand li apart. There is a heavenly rooster on top. When the sun first rises, its light illuminates this tree, and the heavenly rooster crows, followed by all other chickens. This is recorded in the Qimin Yaoshu (Essential Techniques for the Common People), volume 6, the Leiji (Classified Records), volume 91, and the Yuyan (Imperial Inspection), volume 918. There are two deities, the left one named Long and the right one named ○ (unclear). They hold reed ropes to watch for evil spirits and kill them. The above three sentences are added based on the Yuzhu Baojian (Treasure Mirror of the Four Seasons) and the map. Today, people make two peach figures stand at the door, placing rooster feathers in the rope, which is a remnant of the past. This is recorded in the Yuyan (Imperial Inspection), volume 29, and the Yuzhu Baojian (Treasure Mirror of the Four Seasons), volume 1.

    7

    To the east of Penglai lies the mountain of Daiyu, upon which stands the tree of Fusang. The tree is ten thousand feet tall. At the top of the tree, there is often a celestial rooster that builds its nest there. Every night at midnight, the celestial rooster crows, and the sun bird in the middle of the day responds to it; when the sun bird crows, all the chickens in the world also crow. In the Ancient Jade Diagrams (古玉图谱), Volume Twenty-Four.

    To the northwest of Kunlun, there is a mountain, which is described in the Taiping Yulan (御览) as being to the southwest, circling for thirty thousand li, with a giant snake coiling around it, making three complete turns. The snake is nine thousand li long. Above, the White Collection (白帖) Volume Ninety-Eight and the Taiping Yulan Volume Thirty-Eight also mention that the snake lives on this mountain, feeding on the vast ocean. In Leigudao (类聚) Volume Sixty-Six and Yiwen Leiju (广记) Volume Four Hundred Fifty-Six.

    To the southwest of Yumen Pass is where thunder and lightning occur. In the Book of Cuts (书钞), it is mentioned that to the east of the Qiang people's territory, there is a small kingdom with about five or six hundred households, without other duties. There is a mountain within the kingdom, and on top of the mountain, there is a temple. The people of the kingdom annually collect thousands of stones, which they call "Thunder Tips," and offer them to the temple to use for thunder and lightning. The number of stones decreases daily from the spring thunder until they are all gone by autumn. In Taiping Yulan Volumes Seven Hundred Ninety-Seven, Thirteen, and Fifty-Two, and Book of Cuts Volume One Hundred Fifty-Two, and Feng Shi Wen Jian Ji (封氏闻见记) Volume Eight, and Shi Lei Fu Zhu (事类赋注) Volume Seven.

    In the East Sea, there is a dangerous place called Snake Hill, where there is much wet and marshy ground, and many snakes live, but no people. Some of the snakes have human heads and snake bodies. In Leigudao Volume Sixty-Six and Yiwen Leiju Volume Four Hundred Fifty-Six.

    On the mountain of Yuanqiu, there are many large snakes, which are suppressed with male yellow essence (雄黄精). In Taiping Yulan Volume Nine Hundred Eighty-Eight.

    8

    The Great Yuezhi and the Western Hu, a three-character term mentioned in the Shu Chao (Book of Collections) as "日反" (or variations such as "日支牛," "反牛," "日及," and "白皮牛"), which likely refers to a type of ox or a cultural concept. Today, one cuts and takes its flesh, three or four pounds (or two or three pounds, according to Lei Ju), and the next day, the flesh has already returned, and the wound heals immediately (or the wound heals the next day, according to Shi Lei Fu Zhi and Tong Dian). The Huan Yu Ji (Record of the World) also mentions that it is called "复牛" (or "复作及," a variant or error in the text), meaning "cut and regrows." When Han people entered this country, they did not know that the ox was considered a treasure. Han people said, "We have a creature, as big as a finger, called a silkworm, which eats mulberry leaves and spits out silk for humans." Foreigners no longer believe in the existence of silkworms.

    In the West of the Great Tree Mountain, there is a tree that bears flowers. By consuming its fruit, one can understand the languages of all nations.

    In the north of Xuantu, there is a mountain with flowers, and people pick them to spin and weave into cloth.

According to Qi Min Yao Shu (Essential Techniques for the People's Welfare), in Jingyang, the fruit is like a chicken head, and when pressed, it is like sesame seeds, and its juice tastes like pork fat.

    The Ghostly Bird of Yu Zhang

    The Ghostly Bird of Yu Zhang flies at night and hides during the day, and is classified as a spirit or ghost. When it has feathers, it appears as a flying bird; when it sheds its feathers, it transforms into a woman. According to the Bei Hu Lu, this description is placed above the story of the man from Yu Zhang. It is also known by several names: "Daughter of Heaven," "Night-Walking Maiden," "Hook Star," and "Hidden Flyer." The bird does not have its own offspring and often adopts human children, raising them as its own. Today, people do not leave children's clothes exposed at night because this creature marks them with blood to identify them, and then takes the child.
