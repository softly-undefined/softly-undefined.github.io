    A Traditional Saying goes: "In ancient times, there was a couple who, during a famine, died of starvation after eating only vegetables. Both of them transformed into a green and purple phenomenon, hence the common name 'Beautiful Woman Rainbow.' Guo said: 'Red is associated with the rain-making ritual, and it is commonly called 'Beautiful Woman.'"

    During the early years of the Jinyi era, Xue Yuan of Jinling had a rainbow. It drank from the dregs of the pot, and in a moment, it sucked up the sound and vanished. Xue Yuan loaded wine and poured it over it, and as soon as it was poured, it dried up, then spewed gold filling the pot. Thus, disasters were gradually eliminated, and prosperity increased year by year.

    Wen Zhan of Taiyuan had a maid who saw an old woman weeping towards her, with no holes or openings. The maid was terrified and reported to Wen Zhan. Wen Zhan then drew a knife and chased after her, and she transformed into something like a purple rainbow, gracefully stretching out and rising up to the sky.

    Wang Dao's son, Yi Qing, was ill in Guangling. While eating, suddenly a white rainbow entered the room and drank his porridge. Yi Qing threw the utensils onto the steps, and then there was a storm, with the sound reverberating through the hall and doorways. After a long time, it disappeared.

    Mount Hengyang and Mount Jiuyi both have temples dedicated to Shun. Whenever the governor repairs and performs a clean and respectful sacrifice, the sound of stringed instruments and singing can be heard. During the reign of Emperor Zhang of Han, Xiji, a literary figure from Lingling, found a flute at a temple in Lengdao County. It was a white jade tube, presented by the Queen Mother of the West during Shun's time.

    Mount Heng has three peaks, all extremely beautiful. The first is named Huagai and Zigai, the second is named Shiwei, with a stone room at its base where one often hears the sound of recitation, clear and resonant. The third is named Furong, the most remote and rugged, and can only be seen on a clear and bright morning. On the peak, there is a waterfall that flows like a piece of silk, dividing through the green forest and cascading down the mountain.

    In the Luo County of Changsha, there is the river where Qu Yuan threw himself, with its clear waters and bright mountains, unlike any other place. The people built a temple on the western bank of the Mi River, and the flat stone with horse tracks still remains. It is said that on the day Qu Yuan threw himself into the river, he came riding a white horse.

    The Guishi Mountain of Xunyang is located on the bank of the river. At first, when Huán Xuán came westward, he ordered someone to climb the mountain. As they reached the middle of the mountain, they heard a long, clear cry. Upon reaching the summit, they saw a person sitting with his legs spread out on a stone.

    The TianTai Mountain of Kuaiji, though not far away, cannot be ascended unless one is completely detached from form. The Red City blocks the path, and waterfalls strike the way, with the danger of moss and algae, and depths that cannot be fathomed.

    The Bian Mountain of Wucheng, originally named Earth Mountain, has a temple dedicated to Xiang Ji, who called himself King of Bian, and thus the mountain was renamed. At the foot of the mountain is a stone box, several feet high. Chen County's Yin Kang often tried to open it, but when he did, it was met with wind, rain, and darkness, so he stopped.

    The Diaoji Mountain is where Tao Kan once fished. Below the mountain in the water, he found a shuttle, which he hung on the wall. Soon after, there was a thunderstorm, and the shuttle transformed into a red dragon, flying away from the sky. The tracks of Tao Kan still remain on the stones of the mountain.

    The Chengji Mountain overlooks a clear river below. Once, a fisherman stayed by the river and, in the middle of the night, heard the sound of stringed instruments and singing in the water, with the tones of the pentatonic scale being harmonious and smooth, and the clear playing being harmonious and close.

    On Mount Bai Zhang there are stone houses, inside which there are stone tables, on which are placed two stone books.

    In Yongning County, Tao Mountain has a river with red and reddish water, a natural stone bridge, and many unusual aquatic animals. During rainy weather, one can often hear the sound of horns being blown.

    To the west of Liangzhou, there is a sand mountain. Folklore says that in ancient times, an army was defeated here, leaving tens of thousands of corpses. From then on, strong winds carried sand over the area, eventually forming a mountain. It was named Sand Mountain, and sometimes one can hear the sound of drums and horns.

    During the 8th year of the Chih Wu era (245 AD) under the reign of Sun Quan, General Chen Xun was sent to Jurong to transport grain. On the way, they broke through a rock and discovered a black object with no head or tail, shaped like a large boat, about dozens of zhang long, moving sluggishly. After a while, it melted into a liquid, and no one could identify it. Later, a spring source was discovered, and it was believed to be a water vein. During severe droughts, other streams dried up, but this large flow remained.

    In the eastern region, Taihu Lake, during the Gengshen year, a group of five hundred soldiers were about to break a dam. They first offered wine and meat to the gods and promised that the water would dry up. That night, they dreamed of a voice saying, "The pond water will quickly dry up. If you see a large fish, be careful not to kill it. There is also a copper pot, which should not be disturbed." The next morning, the water suddenly dried up, and they found a white fish with an unusual shape. The soldiers, greedy for profit, cut and cooked the fish, finding the food they had offered the previous day filling its intestines. Soon after, they found another copper pot and disturbed it, causing the water to suddenly surge. All five hundred soldiers drowned at once, except for the supervisor, who survived and recounted the events. To this day, the lake is still known as the Five Hundred Pools.

    The Yongjia Prefecture has a place called Bai Bu Lai, where the locals block the water to catch fish, slaughtering live creatures and praying and sacrificing to seek a bountiful catch. After a long time, they got nothing, and the group became angry and resentful, about to give up their business. That night, they all had a dream of an old man who said, "You all may just take a short break and think carefully about what is appropriate." Suddenly at night, they heard jumping sounds, woke up in surprise and looked together, and it turned out to be a big fish, which they made into minced meat and suddenly obtained a hundred pounds, so they named the place Bai Bu Lai after the hundred pounds.

    During the Jin dynasty, Wu Li created a fish barrier in Yun Lake, and a big fish transformed into a human, telling Wu Li, "Later, a big fish will attack the barrier; do not kill it." Wu Li agreed. In a moment, a big fish arrived, followed by a group of fish, and Wu Li and his companions mistakenly killed the big fish. That night, with wind and rain and dark and gloomy weather, all the fish flew up among the trees, and it was therefore called the Flying Fish Path.

    The Lanling Changlu County has a city with a Huashan, on which there is a well, inside of which there is a bird's nest. The bird has a golden beak, black color, and round wings. When this bird is seen, there will be a flood, and the well also cannot be looked into; those who look into it do not live for a year and die immediately.

    Xunyang Tan Chun lived in Changsha, and his house has an ancient well. Every night, they always hear sounds like firecrackers, and it was passed down to be called the dragon's roar.

    The Jurong County has the Temple of Yanling Jizi, and in front of the temple, there is a well and a stream that always boil up, so it is called the Boiling Well. Even now, it is still the same, and it is also called the Boiling Pond.

    Chen Prefecture's Xie Hui, with the courtesy name Xuanming, has an ancient well on the road south of his house. In the second year of the Yuanjia era, those who drew water suddenly saw two dragons very clearly, and passersby stopped to watch, all expressing amazement. Someone went into the well, and found out it was bricks, rising up in the shape of a dragon.

    In the early years of the Yuanjia era, a group of Wuxi barbarians entered a place where deer were being shot and followed the deer into a narrow cave that could only accommodate a person. The barbarians entered the cave and saw a ladder beside it. They climbed the ladder and suddenly found themselves in a bright and open space, with lush mulberry and fruit trees, and people moving about freely without finding it strange. These barbarians marked their path by cutting trees along the way, but later they became disoriented and lost their way.

    In the eastern region of Hedong, there was a man named Wu Qiu Jian, whose courtesy name was Zhong Gong. He once led a campaign against the Wōju people and sent Wang Qi to explore the eastern boundary. The elderly people reported that there had been a broken ship that floated out with the waves. On the coast, there was a man with another face on his neck. When they captured him, he could not communicate and died without eating. They also found a cloth garment floating out of the sea, which looked like clothing worn by Chinese people, but the sleeves were unusually long, extending three zhang (a traditional Chinese unit of length).

    During the Wei dynasty, a large bell in front of the palace rang without reason, and people were puzzled by it. They consulted Zhang Hua, who said, "This is because the copper mountains in Shu Commandery have collapsed, and the bell is responding to it." Soon after, Shu Commandery reported the incident, and it turned out to be exactly as Zhang Hua had said.

    During the reign of Emperor Wu of Jin, a stone emerged from the collapsed bank of the Linping shore in Wu County. When struck with a drum, it made no sound. They consulted Zhang Hua, who said, "You can take the camphor wood from Shu Commandery, carve it into the shape of a fish, and strike it, and it will make a sound." They followed his advice, and the sound could be heard for dozens of li (a traditional Chinese unit of distance).

    During the Jin dynasty, there was a person in the central government who kept a copper basin that rang constantly like someone knocking on it every morning and evening. They consulted Zhang Hua, who said, "This basin is in tune with the palace bell in Luoyang. The palace bell is struck in the morning and evening, so the sound of the basin is in harmony with it. If you make the basin lighter, the sound will be out of tune, and it will stop ringing." They followed his advice, and the basin stopped ringing afterward.

    Yuzhang has a type of stone called "shihuang," which is white in color and has a loose texture. When water is poured over it, it becomes hot, and when a tripod is placed on top, cooking food will make it ready. When the stone cools down, it should be poured with water again. This was observed by Leihuan, who asked Zhang Hua about it. Zhang Hua replied, "This is a burning stone."

    During the Yuan Kang period, someone entered the foot of Mount Song and found a bamboo slip. There were two lines of script on the slip. Officials in the palace passed it around to show each other, but no one knew what it was. Zhang Hua, the Grand Works Minister, asked the scholar Dong , who said, "This is the inscription from the Xianjie Tomb of Emperor Ming." Upon checking, it turned out to be true.

    In the fifth year of the Yuan Kang period of Emperor Huai of Jin, the armory caught fire, burning three items: the sword of Han Gaozu (Emperor Gao) that cut the white snake, the shoes of Confucius, and the head of Wang Mang. Zhang Maoxian, the Chief Clerk of the Secretariat, feared trouble and arranged soldiers for protection. Everyone saw the sword pass through the roof and fly away, and no one knew where it went.

    During the Jianyuan period of Emperor Kang of Jin, a fisherman caught a golden lock while fishing. When he pulled the lock out, he saw a golden ox. He quickly pulled out the ox, which broke, leaving a lock two feet long.

    During the Taoyuan period of Emperor Xiaowu of Jin, Xu Sunjiang from Linwu, Guiyang, saw money overflowing from the bank. He immediately loaded it onto his boat, and soon it all turned into earth.

    During the Yixi period of Emperor An of Jin, Pang Yi served as the Prefect of Yidu and kept people and horses in the fields. He saw a copper stove with a handle and a lock, moving. He brought it back to show Pang Yi. He then placed it in a cage and sent it to the north of Jingzhou, which was not the capital. Suddenly there was wind and rain, with a sound, and fire lit up the sky, coming straight to the ship, and the stove was lost.

    8

    During the reign of Emperor Xiaowu of Liu Song (403–424 AD), Huang Shu of Xinye found a shipload of gold while farming. A diviner advised him, "Do not use it for three years; it will bring long-term wealth." Huang Shu did not follow the advice, and the gold eventually became part of the soil.

    During the Jin dynasty, a large ship was discovered in the Qiantang River near Zhanglin. Anyone who boarded the ship would experience uncontrollable drifting and swaying. The ship's official, Zhang Yue, was the only one who could control it. After Zhang Yue's death, the ship became uncontrollable.

    During the reign of Emperor Taihe of Liu Song (402–420 AD), two large ships with coins were discovered while building a granary in Shanyin County. The coins had patterns on them. As night fell, the workers rushed to inform the authorities, who sent guards to protect the site. By the next morning, the coins were missing, but the ships remained. Upon inspection, the ships appeared to be full of coins.

    During the reign of Emperor Fu Jian of the Former Qin dynasty (357–385 AD), a woodcutter in Chang'an found a golden tripod inside the city walls. He reported it to the emperor, who sent someone to retrieve it. Upon arrival, the tripod had turned into copper. When it entered the palace, it transformed into a large bell.

    A bell in the western river would ring on the first and last days of the lunar month, producing a sorrowful and intense sound that made travelers feel sad.

    In Huaiyuan County, there was a river called Yuanma with a copper ship. By the riverbank was a shrine containing a green pearl. If not properly worshipped, taking the pearl would be unlucky.

    In Changshan, Zhuo and his wife were collecting herbs by the stream when they saw two copper cauldrons floating downstream. They took them home. The cauldrons had lids full of copper objects that shone brightly and made a natural sound. Zhuo was afraid and buried the lid in the north mountain. He then sold the cauldrons to others. They loaded them onto a boat to sell as goods, but the boat capsized without reason, and the cauldrons were lost.
