    Liu Ting
    Liu Shao Bao Ting, courtesy name Xingwu. As a general, he lived at home. At that time, bandits suddenly rose up in Ningzhou, their power greatly expanded. The provincial governor sent the prefect and the governor to request help, but Liu Shao Bao refused on the grounds of illness. The governor then sent other officials to persuade him, but he still refused. All of them were very angry. They ordered a doctor to go and verify his condition; if he was faking, they would report him. When the doctor arrived, Liu Shao Bao was lying weakly in bed. The officials were anxious and had no solution. Soon after, they suddenly received a report that General Liu had defeated the bandits and returned. They were greatly startled and said, "We couldn't have known the general would go, so why did he use such cunning?" Liu Shao Bao replied, "The bandits are descendants of Chen Youliang, who had been plotting for generations to seize power. If they had known I would come, they would have been well-prepared, so I attacked them secretly. This is a military strategy." Everyone then understood.

    At that time, there was a strong emphasis on literature, and during official gatherings, Liu Shao Bao was seated below the scholars. During a public banquet organized by the local gentry, the funds were insufficient. They would call out to Liu Shao Bao by his courtesy name, saying, "Xingwu, you handle this." Liu Shao Bao always contributed ten or a hundred times more than the others. Sometimes, when he was drunk, he would order his servants to ride horses to entertain the guests. When Liu Shao Bao got excited, he would often get on his horse and dance with two swords. The spectators only saw white mist swirling and blinding their eyes, unable to see his face. Although they were amazed by his skill, they also treated it as a form of entertainment.

The others took turns toasting him, and they drank heavily, becoming quite drunk. After sending Huang Gong to bed, they specially set up a small bed with pillows, quilts, and mats. He then told Gu to use the mat for sleeping. The mat was thick and narrow, making it difficult to turn over, so he instructed her to use the quilt instead. Gu then came close to Huang Gong, and he calmly said, "It is unnecessary." He turned his body inward, took a few deep breaths, and fell asleep. After several days, his appearance became more robust. On the eve of his execution, an old prison guard held needles and thread and wept, saying, "This is the final service I can offer to my master!"     5

    The Historian said: This is a record of the extraordinary deeds of Mr. Huang, as documented in The Wangxi Anthology, alongside the story of Left Zhongyi Gong. From ancient times, loyal ministers and righteous men have always taken the world as their responsibility. Even in times of chaos and transformation, they would still strive to stabilize the nation with their own strength, and if necessary, sacrifice their lives for the country, believing that they could not bear the blame for their duties. Therefore, when they calmly accepted their fate, even the names of loyalty and righteousness were not spoken, and what concern was there for their families or personal affairs, let alone the temptations of pleasure, wealth, and material gain? I have read Buddhist texts, and Kasyapa said, "The body of a Buddha is indestructible by the fires of the world." The Yogacara school also states, "Mara has four daughters, who are extremely beautiful. They came before the Bodhisattva, displaying various seductive poses. The Bodhisattva, with his moral strength and mental discipline, transformed the four daughters into old and ugly women, causing them to retreat in shame." This shows that reason cannot overcome desire, which can even make the most courageous and intelligent people lose their virtue and wisdom, but only immortals and Buddhas can control it. However, while immortals and Buddhas are completely unmoved, sages and virtuous people can be both active and passive. In the case of Left Gong, who suffered such a severe misfortune, his few solemn words still resonate with vitality today. If he had always been as refined and cultured as the Gu family, and if he had lived in close intimacy with them, how could it have moved him even slightly? This is the intention of the historian to uncover hidden truths and clarify subtle meanings. As for Mr. Huang's final words, they were determined by fate, just as with the two individuals. But was this their original intention? This is something that can make one weep for the two individuals.

    In assessing Hong Chengchou's abilities, it seems he was still capable of handling military affairs among the officials of late Ming. The recorded achievements in the historical records are also noteworthy. However, Huang Zongxi once criticized the exaggeration of these recorded achievements, suggesting that they are not entirely reliable. According to Yanpo Zaji (A Miscellany of Exposure), when Hong Chengchou suffered a defeat, his family members at home had already engraved his biography and distributed it to mourners. At the same time, Emperor Chongzhen was conducting sacrifices at the 14 altars, and news of Hong Chengchou's surrender reached the court. Later, Jin Sheng led a rebellion in Huizhou, and his disciple Jiang Tianyi also joined him in defeat. Hong Chengchou ordered them to surrender, and Jiang Tianyi recited the imperial eulogy to shame him. Afterward, when Hong Chengchou returned to the Qing court and passed away in Beijing, his family members engraved another biography, no longer mentioning his previous loyalty to the Ming dynasty, starting instead from his entry into the Qing court. A frivolous person obtained both biographies, compiled them into one book, and used it as a source of amusement.

    The History of Ming also states: In the 14th year of the reign of Emperor Chongzhen, the Qing forces surrounded Songshan. Hong Chengchou and Qiu Minyang swore to die in defense, but no external aid arrived, and their supplies of fodder and grain were exhausted. By the second month of the following year, they had already been besieged for half a year. Unable to hold out any longer, the city fell, and Hong Chengchou surrendered. Qiu Minyang refused to surrender and died. He was posthumously granted the title of Right Vice Minister of Judicial Inspection, received the imperial sacrifice at six altars, and the government arranged for his burial. The emperor ordered the construction of a temple in the capital, placing him alongside Hong Chengchou. The emperor intended to personally attend the sacrifice, but upon hearing of Hong Chengchou's surrender, he decided against it.

    The Quan Xie Shan Ji records: A person from Shining named Ni told me that his father had once seen a book titled Qiu Cao Ri Lu from his ancestors' lineage while visiting the Tancheng family in the village of Cangqiao. This person served as a prison official during the reign of Emperor Xizong. The book documented the cases of virtuous individuals killed by Wei Zhongxian, who were not sent to the Eastern Factory but rather to the Ministry of Justice.

    Ni recounted that Xuanye, upon entering prison, had every drink and meal reported to Wei Zhongxian by the prison officials. However, Xuanye showed no unusual reaction. He always slept with a rattan pillow, regardless of the season, and never left it. Every night when the surroundings were quiet, he would perform a ritual of bowing twice to the North Star, offering the rattan pillow to it, though no one could understand the meaning behind this. When asked about it, Xuanye merely smiled and did not respond. Eventually, the day of his execution arrived, and Xuanye remained calm, presenting a final memorial to the emperor, still discussing border affairs, and composing a farewell poem. The draft of his memorial was intercepted by the officials of the Ministry of Justice, who said, "How can a prisoner submit a memorial?" Xuanye replied, "This is Zhao Gao's words." (Note: Twelve characters are missing here.) "How could the holy court allow such a thing?" He then calmly accepted his fate.

    At the time, there was an order to send his head to the nine border regions. When the officials of the Ministry of Justice recorded his execution, they found the execution ground empty, with only the rattan pillow remaining. They were greatly alarmed and warned each other not to reveal what had happened. They secretly reported to Wei Zhongxian, who ordered the interrogation of members of the Xiong family. After a thorough search, they found nothing. Wei Zhongxian, having no other options, kept the matter secret. The heads sent to the nine border regions were not the real ones. After Wei Zhongxian's downfall, Prince Zhao Bi submitted multiple memorials requesting the return of Xuanye's head to be buried in Puzhou, knowing full well that it was not the real head, but using it to resolve this unjust case.
