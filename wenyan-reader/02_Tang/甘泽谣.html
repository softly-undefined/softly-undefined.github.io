    Wei Xiansheng

    Wei Xiansheng was born during the Zhou Dynasty and lived in the Song region. Beyond Confucian texts, he studied musical compositions. In the early years of the Sui Dynasty, he traveled to the region west of the pass, where the Grand Music Master was examining music. The debate was not settled, and upon hearing that Mr. Wei had arrived, they all went to consult him. Mr. Wei then took the instruments from the conquered Chen Dynasty and, together with the music officials Su Kui and Cai Ziyuan, carefully examined their pitch and rhythm. Then, the percussion, strings, and wind instruments were all properly tuned. He was appointed to the Qing Shang Shu (Office of Music and Entertainment) as a Taile Guan (High-ranking Music Official) and received 200 pieces of silk as payment. Mr. Wei did not re-enter public service and returned to the Song region, entertaining himself with music and wine.

    By the end of the Sui Dynasty, civil war broke out, and Yang Xuangan was defeated. His chief strategist, Li Mi, fled to Yanmen, changing his name and beginning to teach. Mr. Wei was from the same hometown and, therefore, they began to visit each other. They often discussed the principles of music, and Li Mi was quite skilled. Mr. Wei then teased him, saying, "I see that you are dejected and confused, your mind is unsettled, and your speech is restrained. Dejection means recent defeat, confusion means having no support, an unsettled mind means an unsettled spirit, and restraint in speech means thinking of seeking help from others."
