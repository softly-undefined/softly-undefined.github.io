    The Four Treasuries, Subcategory Twelve
    Records of the Great Tang
    Category One: Miscellaneous Events
    Summary

    We respectfully submit that the Records of the Great Tang is a single volume whose author's name is unknown. It records various events from the early Tang Dynasty to the reign of Emperor Yuanhe. Neither the bibliographies of the Tang nor the Song Dynasties list this work. It has a preface written by the author, who states that in the summer of the eighth year, he traveled south to the mountainous regions. On leisure days, while traveling by boat, he recorded what he heard. Upon examination, it is found that after the reign of Emperor Mu, only the reigns of Emperor Taizong, Emperor Dazong, and Emperor Xiantong had an eighth year. This book does not specify the era name, and it is also unclear when the eighth year refers to. The records of the deeds and words of Tang nobles and officials are quite detailed, and many of these records are used in historical works. Occasionally, it includes humorous anecdotes and trivial matters from court and common life, which often differ from other works. It mentions that during the reign of Emperor Chengyuan, Princesses Zhengguo and Haunguo were given posthumous titles, marking the beginning of such posthumous titles for princesses. However, it does not mention that Princess Pingyang, daughter of Emperor Gaozu, already had a posthumous title. Additionally, it recounts a story about Xiang Yingshi encountering an old man who said he resembled Prince Poyang. According to the Book of Strange Stories, the old man was actually a notorious thief, but here he is recorded as a strange person. This discrepancy arises because different versions were circulating at the time, and the author recorded what he heard, which inevitably led to contradictions. However, the parts that can be relied upon are about six or seven out of ten. This work was submitted for official review in June of the forty-fourth year of Emperor Qianlong's reign. [1]

    《书》云：“不有博弈者乎？犹贤乎已。”这是圣人对饱食终日而无所作为的深恶痛绝。又说：“吾不试，故艺。”这里的“艺”不仅仅指多才多艺，而是因为这些技艺在当时没有实际用处，只能在困顿愁苦之时有所记录。八年夏天，南行至岭南，闲暇之时，乘船顺流而下，沿途听闻了许多故事，便将其记录下来，故名为《传载》。虽为小说，但或有可观看之处，让人或喜或笑。

    杜河南（杜佑）常常收藏书籍达万卷之多，每卷之后必定有自题：“清俸买来手自校，汝曹读之知圣道。鬻及借人为不孝。”

    阳道州（阳城）在担任朝官时，家境十分贫困，常将木枕和布被典当以换取钱财，许多人争相购买。

    苏州开元寺东侧有一户陆姓人家世代居住，门前临河，有一块巨石矗立，相传是吴国陆绩担任郁林郡太守卸任后，因船只轻便，便用此石压重，人称“郁林石”。陆氏家族从陆绩到后代子孙，包括国朝太子少保兖公，都守护着这处居所。如今，子孙逐渐衰落，仅存十分之一。
