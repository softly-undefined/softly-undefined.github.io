    We officials respectfully present the Miscellaneous Records of Emperor Ming in two volumes and the Supplementary Records in one volume, compiled by Zheng Chuhui of the Tang dynasty. Zheng Chuhui, courtesy name Yanmei, was from Yingyang and the grandson of the chancellor Zheng Yuqing. Zhao Gongwu counts both the main text and the supplementary volume together, while Ye Mengde in his Summer Retreat Notes says that Zheng Chuhui's Miscellaneous Records of Emperor Ming records the dispute between Zhang Qujiang and Li Linfu over the real estate of Niu Xianke. At that time, it was autumn, and the emperor ordered Gao Lishi to give a white feather fan to Zhang Qujiang, who, feeling anxious, wrote a poem to present. It seemed to say that Emperor Ming was about to be dismissed for disobeying orders, hence the gift of the fan in autumn to convey his intention. The New Book of Tang included this event in the biography.

Hépú then told Fang, "When you die, it will be because you ate fish. After your death, you will be buried in a coffin made of paulownia wood. After several days of illness, the magistrate of the prefecture suddenly prepared fish and invited Fang to his official residence. Fang happily agreed and went there.
