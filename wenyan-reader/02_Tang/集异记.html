According to the Artistic Literature Record of the Tang Dynasty, Xue Yongruo, also known as Zhongsheng, was a governor of Guangzhou during the Longqing period, but his place of origin is not recorded. According to the Reading Records of Zhao Gongwu, the first story recorded is about Xu Zuoqing's transformation, and this edition also places this story first, which aligns with Zhao's records. It is likely still the original version. The stories commonly known include those of Di Renjie's green fur coat, Wang Wei's black robe, and others. [1]

    Grand Secretary Di Renjie
    Grand Secretary Di Renjie had a natural aptitude for medicine and was exceptionally skilled in acupuncture. During the reign of Emperor Gaozong (era name Xianqing), he responded to imperial command to enter the capital. On his journey, he passed through the northern part of Huazhou, where the market was bustling with many people gathered like a wall. Grand Secretary Di Renjie pulled his reins and looked far into the distance, seeing a large sign with big letters that said, "Whoever can treat this child will receive a thousand bolts of silk." He immediately went to see it. There was a child from a wealthy family, around 14 or 15 years old, lying under the sign. The child had a growth on the tip of his nose, as big as a fist or stone, with the base attached to the nose, only as thick as a chopstick. If touched, it caused extreme pain, piercing to the bone. Therefore, his two eyes were constrained by the growth, turning white, and he was in a critical condition, about to die. Grand Secretary Di Renjie felt moved with compassion for a long time and said, "I can do it." The child's parents and relatives knelt to the ground in prayer, and immediately carried a thousand bolts of silk and placed them beside his seat. Grand Secretary Di Renjie then ordered the child to be lifted up and inserted a needle about an inch deep at the back of the head. He asked the patient, "Has the effect of the needle reached the affected area?" The patient nodded in agreement. Grand Secretary Di Renjie immediately pulled out the needle, and the growth fell off instantly. The child's eyes immediately returned to normal, and he felt no pain. His parents and relatives wept and bowed, presenting silk goods to him. Grand Secretary Di Renjie smiled and said, "I pity your life in great danger. I acted out of urgency to save the child. I am not a seller of skills." He did not look back and left. [25]
