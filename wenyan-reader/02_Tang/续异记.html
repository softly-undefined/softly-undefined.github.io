    The Continuation of Strange Records (续异记) Anonymous

    In the Eastern Han dynasty, Huangmenlang Xiao Shiyi was executed in the second year of Emperor He's Yongyuan era. Several days before, a dog that had been regularly kept in the household came to the woman's presence and spoke to her, saying, "You have no good fortune; your family will soon be ruined, what shall we do?" The woman remained silent and did not show fear. The dog left after a short while. When Xiao Shiyi returned home, the woman continued to imitate the dog's speech. Before she finished, the authorities arrived to arrest her. (Recorded in the Taiping Guangji, Volume 141)

    During the reign of Emperor Xiaowu of Jin, Xu Miao served as a Zhongshu Shilang (a mid-level official in the central government). People around him often sensed that Xu Miao was alone in his tent, as if he were conversing with someone. An old student of his once watched secretly one night and saw nothing. When the sky was faintly lit, he opened the window and suddenly saw something fly out from behind the screen and dart straight into an iron pot. He followed it and found nothing else but a cluster of cattail roots with a large green grasshopper beneath them. Although he suspected it was a spirit, he had never heard of such a thing before, so he merely picked off its two wings. That night, it entered Xu Miao's dream and said, "Trapped by your disciple, the path of coming and going is cut off; although close, it feels like mountains and rivers." Xu Miao was deeply moved by the dream. The student realized the meaning and gently hinted at it. Xu Miao initially doubted and did not immediately speak. He told the student, "When I first arrived to serve, I saw a young woman in green clothes pass by, still wearing two buns, very beautiful in appearance. I lightly teased her, and she came to me. I liked her and became infatuated. I also did not know where she came from." He also shared the dream with the student. The student then reported the entire situation, and Xu Miao no longer pursued or killed the grasshopper. (Recorded in the Taiping Guangji, Volume 473)

    During the reign of Emperor Xiaowu of Jin, in the year 405, there was a man named Shi Ziran from Lingling who, despite being born into a humble family, possessed a sharp and perceptive mind. His family owned a large farm, and he built a small, makeshift shelter near the fields to monitor the crops, where he would often stay overnight. One night, while he was still awake, he saw a man approach. The man was of average height, dressed in a simple yellow linen robe, and walked straight up to Shi Ziran, raising his hands in greeting. Shi Ziran asked the man his name, and the man replied, "My surname is Lu, and my given name is Gou. My home is by the Zong Stream, beside the water." A few days later, one of the farm workers dug into the ant hill on the western side of the field and suddenly discovered a large pit filled with mole crickets, nearly a peck in quantity, with several particularly large ones, one of which was especially massive. Shi Ziran then realized, "The guest named Lu Gou, whose name sounds like 'mole cricket.' His home by the Zong Stream is the western pit." He then poured boiling water into the pit, and thus the mole crickets were finally eradicated.

    Liu Mu dreamed that someone was calling out to meet Liu Zhenjun. In the morning, he divined it and said, "I am going to die. Is there even a Liu Zhenjun now?" Later, Emperor Wu of Liu Song sent someone to welcome him, and together they established the great cause. At that time, Emperor Wu was the General of the Army. Guangji 276.

    Yu Liang of Wuxing was appointed as the Military Governor in the eighth year of Yongming. While lying in his usual sleeping place, he heard the sound of sheep. He suspected it to be a supernatural phenomenon. Peering through the door, he saw a sheep under his bed, about two feet tall, with red-colored fur, its radiance filling the room. Chuxueji 29, Liuji 96, Yuelan 902.

    Liu Zhao of Zhongshan, the magistrate of Moling, was the supervisor of Jiankang in the third year of Tianjian. While cooking with his disciples, he found a turtle in the stove, about a foot long, which was not harmed by the ashes. Liu held a vegetarian feast and released it into the Louhu. Soon after, Liu Zhao was promoted to the position of magistrate of Moling. Guangji 118.

    Liu Xingdao of Guangling, the governor of Lingling, retired to his study after leaving office. He placed his bed against the western wall, but suddenly saw an eye appear on the eastern wall. Soon, there were four eyes, and they gradually increased in number until the room was full; after a while, they disappeared without a trace. He also saw hair growing from the ground, which became more numerous over time. He saw a head emerge, which turned out to be the head of a Fangxiang, and it vanished suddenly. Liu was filled with fear and anxiety, falling ill and unable to get up. Guangji 141.

    Yan Wu Yu, the magistrate of Wuxi, Jinling, stored grain, which later opened up to become snake grass. He burned it, causing poverty. (Recorded in Yuyan 190)

    King Jingling Duan, also referred to as Liu Duan in Guangji, had his attendants around Guangling. In his sleep, he dreamed of someone telling him that the official needed his hair for a spear flag. Upon waking, he found his hair gone. This happened to dozens of people. (Recorded in Yuyan 341 and Guangji 276)

    Huang, the wife of Sun, suddenly saw a child in front of her. He threw something at her, and it leaped into the clouds and disappeared. At night, she heard a song outside the door: "In the past, it filled the tomb of the Xia family, carting mud to their heads to make them bald. Now it resides in Huang's home, unintentionally hurting my eyes." She searched in the nest and found a white swallow, whose left eye was injured. (Recorded in Shilie Fu 19 and Yuyan 922)

    Zhu Fagong of Shanyin once traveled and rested under an orange tree east of Taicheng. Suddenly, a woman appeared, about sixteen or seventeen years old, with a very beautiful appearance. Late in the afternoon, she sent a maid to inform Fagong, saying she wanted to stay overnight as evening approached. After the hour of people had settled, she came. She introduced herself as surnamed Tan, living on the outskirts of the city. They slept together until dawn and then she left. The next day, she came again. This happened for several nights. Every morning when she left, the maid would come to greet her. There was also a boy, about six or seven years old, who was very handsome and lovely; the woman said he was her brother. The next morning when she left, the woman's skirt opened, revealing a turtle's tail and feet. Fagong then realized she was a spirit and wanted to seize her. As evening approached, she came again. He immediately lit a fire to search for her, but she was gone. (Recorded in Guangji 469)
