    ● Stone True Consorts

    There are two goddesses at Zixiao Rock in Luoyuan, known as the Stone True Consorts, whose divine presence is quite renowned. During the early period of the Yongle Emperor's reign, when the military was sailing out to sea, soldiers such as Zhang Yuanhui were traveling at night over the mountains. They encountered two sedan chairs, accompanied by several maids holding lanterns in front. Zhang Yuanhui and his companions suspected it was a wedding procession and set down their burdens to wait. Suddenly, the procession disappeared, and they realized it was the two goddesses. They bowed and thanked them before passing by. The two goddesses were originally daughters of the Xu family in Luoyuan. The elder sister was named Moonlight, and the younger sister was named Snowy. Both were beautiful and knowledgeable in literature and history. At the end of the Five Dynasties, the Green Turban rebels in Chuzhou rose up in rebellion, and the two sisters were captured. They chose to die rather than be dishonored and threw themselves into the river one after the other. In the Song dynasty, a filial son named Gao Zun encountered a jade maiden while gathering firewood in the mountains. She was dressed in a clear and dignified manner and entered his home respectfully. She invited him to drink tea for a long time. Moonlight recited: "In times of chaos and famine, bandits rise up, and even the ants are trying to escape."

During the Yongle era, in the 35th year (1438), a scholar divined for the autumn examination and received the following message: "A single point of jade falls from the clouds, a beautiful woman is incomplete. There was a person who admired his talent and wanted to marry his daughter to him, but worried that he would not be able to support himself.     In the seventh month of the fifth year of the Jingta era, Ningde suffered a severe drought. The people of the county prayed for rain at a western location (the exact name is unclear), but the petition was not well-written.     "We humbly present our sincere hearts and humbly address the dragon's face, requesting the deity to understand our simple hearts and to open its holy eyes. Reflecting on our lives and dwellings, we recognize that the foundation of governance lies in agriculture. We have received the great favor of heaven and earth and appreciate the kindness of rain and dew. In spring, we plow and weed, never shirking the hard work of treading soil. In autumn, we harvest and store, truly hoping to support ourselves. Unfortunately, this year unexpectedly faced prolonged heat, starting from the beginning of the ninth summer, until the beginning of the third autumn, for sixty days without rain. Knowing that the calamity is due to the sins of the common people, we acknowledge that the disaster is truly caused by the heavenly god. Humbly choosing a specific time, we respectfully go to the dragon pool, humbly seeking holy water, hoping for sweet rain to slightly save the withered crops. Humbly hoping the dragon's face is greatly pleased, we request even a drop from the golden vase of the tortoise, fully nourishing the fields with abundant rain.     In Fuan, there was a custom that women who gave birth to daughters were not allowed to raise them.
