    The Lake When It Was First Formed

    The Lake When It Was First Formed is located fifty li north of the current county, with a length of twelve li from north to south and six li from east to west. According to ancient traditions passed down through generations, when the land first began to sink, a woman gave birth to a creature that resembled a dragon or sea monster. When the creature was washed into the water, the land sank, and it moved northeast. Today, there is the Maogang River, which directly connects to Lake Tai. Recently, a stone inscription was discovered, which is the tombstone of Lord Lu of Wu Prefecture during the Tang Dynasty, buried in Qijing Township, Haisalt County, Suzhou, at the Lake When It Was First Formed. Thus, the name of the lake is ancient. Some say that the Parrot Island Gazetteer does not mention this lake, suggesting that it might have existed before the land sank. In the past, a fisherman found a long iron chain in the lake, so long that it nearly capsized his boat, and he discarded it out of fear. Some say that the chain was attached to the creature. From the Han Dynasty to the present, over a thousand years have passed, and the lake has gradually become shallower while the land has gradually increased in size. Occasionally, people have reportedly seen traces of the creature's remains in the lake.
