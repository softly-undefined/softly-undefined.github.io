    湖山叙游 [明]刘暹

    叙湖记
    湖景丽于昼，湖情暇于夜。主一舟焉，以司昼夜之长。晴开雨泊，傍树依云，栩栩然也。虽然，犹未也。神飘忽而无所着，其为游也，不静矣。于是舍舟而僦居。半在山，而半在水。背高而凭流，浣以红，而沃以青。眠食于其间，徘徊于其上。极朝昏之变态，看烟云之吐吞。其于湖也，亦非泛泛焉者矣！虽然，亦未也。湖之境有，未历湖之情，终未周也。于是，乃以一加一杖出没于堤桥烟水之中。加兴杖止，朗止杖兴，任心意之所如，指晴雨以作辍。无刻不以游为事，无日不与湖相周。力之所到，必逐境而寻求。力所未到，亦望焉而生羡。倘佯尽日，心目茫然。

    Narrative of a Journey Through Lakes and Mountains [Ming] Liu Xian

    Narrative of the Lake
    The beauty of the lake is most striking during the day, and its leisurely mood is most evident at night. Managing a boat to oversee the long hours of day and night, sailing in clear weather and anchoring in rain, resting by trees and leaning on clouds, all of which is vividly alive. Although this is so, it is not yet complete. The spirit wanders and has no fixed point, making the journey restless. Therefore, leaving the boat, I rented a dwelling. Half in the mountains and half by the water, back against the high ground and leaning on the flowing water, washed with red and drenched with green. Sleeping and eating within it, wandering above it, observing the changes from morning to evening, watching the clouds rise and fall. In relation to the lake, it is not a superficial experience! Yet, it is still not complete. Although the scenery of the lake is seen, the feelings of the lake have not been fully experienced. Thus, I would take a walking stick and wander through the bridges and waters of the lake. When兴致来了，停下脚步；兴致消退，继续前行，任由心和意自由游走，根据天气决定是否出发。每时每刻都以游历为事，每天都在与湖相伴。力量所及之处，必定追随景色而寻求；力量所不及之处，也会心生向往。整日游荡，心中茫然。

    叙山记

    Those who visit the lake depart from Qiantang, moor at Nanping, and enter Lihu before returning, taking it as the pinnacle of scenery. There are those who have a penchant for something and wish to leave the lake and go to the mountains, no more than going to Lingyin, returning via the Six Bridges and the Three Pools Mirroring the Moon. People never fail to think it strange. I am surprised by it.

    I asked Zhao Ziyong, "Now I wish to explore the mountains. Is there a way to slightly change the scenery and enchant my eyes and mind?" Zhao Ziyong replied, "To the south, there are the southern peaks; to the north, there are the northern peaks. The scenic spots converge like spokes of a wheel on the limbs and back, and if counted by months, it could be exhausted!" At the time, I thought his words were good, but I was ill and could not go.

    Ten days later, my illness lifted and my spirit cleared. The new autumn had just arrived, and the cool air washed over me, making my weak body feel light and wanting to fly. The clear air from the two peaks circulated between my clothes and shoes; the cloud light from the myriad valleys floated above my eyes and mind.

    近俯湖上诸峰，则怒生丛指，若花蕊之初开；远眺江上诸岫，则奔走披离，带烟光而明媚。汀水随秋以共回，湖波吹碧而无休。遥望一寺于飞峰之巅。耸然自振，以通江湖之气为五云端。逾五云端，寻山蹊而返。历险（山献），杂荒芜。麋鹿为群，猴狖相狎。目不得停，足不得住。壁立以抵山根，若从云端而坠者，为十八涧之首。从此而涉清流，履白石，两山翠合。众响相传。桥屡过而不知，涧往来以相送。山穷水尽，湖湛然而明于前者，为十八涧之终。目纷纭于古碧浮青，耳摇乱于湍流树响，鼻惶惑于岚气花香。手忆攀援，足悬登跻。

    11

    Looking down upon the peaks on the lake, they rise up like a cluster of fingers, similar to the petals of a flower just beginning to bloom; looking far into the hills on the river, they rush and scatter, adorned with the glow of smoke and bright. The waters of the shore follow the autumn and return together, the lake waves blowing green and ceaselessly. Looking far into a temple on the summit of a soaring peak, it rises up with a sense of elevation, connecting the breath of the rivers and lakes as the five clouds. Beyond the five clouds, following the mountain path and returning, passing through dangerous terrain, mixed with wilderness, deer gathering in groups, monkeys and apes playing together, the eyes and feet unable to stop, standing like a wall to the base of the mountain, as if falling from the clouds, being the beginning of the Eighteen Gorges. From here, crossing clear streams, treading on white stones, the green of the two mountains merging, sounds echoing each other, crossing bridges repeatedly without noticing, the gorges coming and going, welcoming each other, the mountains and waters reaching their end, the lake clear and bright in front, being the end of the Eighteen Gorges. The eyes dazzled by the ancient blue floating green, the ears confused by the rushing water and tree sounds, the nose bewildered by the misty air and floral fragrance. The hands recalling the climbing, the feet suspended in the act of ascending.

    意有所会，作息因之。或临风而振衣，或踞石而披跣。或画然而啸，或含悽而吟。或效笑于松竹之中，或疾呼于泉瀑之下。过溪桥而恍恍，望岩谷以悠悠。凉叶思红，空林恋绿。群峰寂寂。依素秋以为魂；湿翠盈盈，仗湖光而自定。时见寒潭之集影，时闻跫然之一声。怀抱伊何，语言皆塞。我其为新愈之人乎！我其为久旅之人乎！我其为深山之人乎！我其为清秋之人乎！笑而不答，人不能知。愀以无言，己亦不解。悲古今之不一，慨心事之非遥。恍情性之皆移，觉肺肝之无主。南峰北峰之间，亦岂止幻吾心目而已！耶因记之，以自识焉尔。

    Explanation

    12

    The mind has something to grasp, and actions and rest follow this. Sometimes standing in the wind and flapping one's clothes, sometimes sitting on a rock and exposing one's feet. Sometimes suddenly shouting, sometimes quietly singing with a sense of melancholy. Sometimes laughing among the pines and bamboos, sometimes shouting loudly under waterfalls. Crossing a stream bridge in a dazed state, looking at the rocks and valleys with a sense of leisure. Cool leaves thinking of redness, empty forests longing for green. The peaks are silent, relying on the pure autumn as a soul; wet greenness is abundant, relying on the lake's light to find one's own calm. Sometimes seeing the gathering shadows in a cold pool, sometimes hearing a single sound of footsteps. What is in one's heart? Words are all blocked. Am I a newly healed person? Am I a long-traveling person? Am I a person of the deep mountains? Am I a person of the clear autumn? Smiling but not answering, others cannot understand. Grieving in silence, even oneself does not understand. Lamenting the differences between past and present, regretting that one's inner thoughts are not far away. Feeling that one's emotions and nature have all shifted, feeling that one's heart and liver have no master. Between the southern and northern peaks, it is not just an illusion for my eyes and mind! Thus I record it, to recognize myself.
