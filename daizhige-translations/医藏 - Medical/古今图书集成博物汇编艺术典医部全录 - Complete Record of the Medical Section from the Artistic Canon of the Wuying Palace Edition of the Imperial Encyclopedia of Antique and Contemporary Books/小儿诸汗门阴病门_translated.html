    Sweating in Children

    Direct Instructions for Children (Song, Qian Yi)

    Discussion on Sweating

    Sweating due to joy occurs when the child wears thick clothing and sweats from the forehead; nocturnal sweating happens when the muscles are weak and sweat occurs during sleep, treat it with the Stopping Sweat Powder. General sweating can be treated with the Fragrant Melon Pill. For sweating due to stomach deficiency, which can reach from the neck down to the navel, use the Beneficial Yellow Powder. Sweating from Six Yang Deficiency, affecting from the head down to the neck, is difficult to treat.

    Notes: The above conditions may arise because a child's vital energy is not fully developed, leading to loose pores, or due to food stagnation causing heat, or from heavy clothing and warmth leading to internal heat and excessive fluid loss. Joyful sweating pertains to heart heat; use the Guiding Red Powder for blood heat, and if there is blood deficiency, use the Round Ginseng Decoction. Nocturnal sweating concerns the kidney energy; use the Five Flavor Adaptogenic Powder for false heat, and Human Ginseng Nourishing Decoction if there is Qi and blood weakness. If cold water attacks the earth element, use the Beneficial Yellow Powder. If treatments are indiscriminately applied and harm the spleen and stomach, they may lead to digestive issues. Sweating from Six Yang deficiency is a matter of insufficient constitution, though using the Ginseng and Atractylodes Decoctions can help some recover. Generally, excessive sweating leads to loss of Yang, often resulting in arched bow and stiff neck; using the Ten Full and Great Supplementing Decoction can lead to recovery.[1p]
    Head Sweat

    Sweat is the liquid of the heart. Only head sweat does not require treatment. Children have a pure yang body, and the head is where all yang energies converge, with the heart belonging to fire. Head sweat is a sign of heat rising. Thus, head sweat signifies the excess of clear yang and does not need treatment.

    Spontaneous Sweat

    Spontaneous sweat continuously emerges, day and night. This is due to both blood and qi being hot, with both the nutritive and protective qi being deficient. The treatment should be Dang Gui Liu Huang Decoction. The formula includes Astragalus to replenish the protective qi, Dang Gui and Rehmannia to nourish the nutritive qi, and Scutellaria, Coptis, and Rhizoma Rhei to drain the heat of qi and blood. Floating wheat is used to guide it into the lungs to dissipate the heat in the skin and hair. This is the essential formula for treating all types of sweat.

    Night Sweat

    Night sweat emerges during dreams and dries up upon waking. Its disease is in the kidneys, and it is advisable to use Dang Gui Liu Huang Decoction with a stopping sweat powder for treatment.[2p]
    Zhong Yang said: When a person experiences night sweats, it indicates that sweating occurs while asleep, suggesting a deficiency in the muscles. The Tuan Shen Decoction treats children's night sweats and spontaneous sweating. Sometimes, an excess of heart blood can also cause sweating; this medicine helps to consolidate heart energy. Tang's theory states: Sweat is blood. Blood deficiency can also lead to spontaneous sweating and heat; it is advisable to take Tuan Shen Decoction, Oyster Powder, or Long Dan Decoction to treat all types of children's night sweats. There is also Tong Shen Pill to treat excessive sweating throughout the body. Young children are often weak in essence and qi, and tend to have excess heat in their nature. If they wear heavy clothing, overconsume hot foods, or suffer from major illnesses that exhaust their fluids, their yang energy may become excessive. When water cannot control fire, internal organs accumulate heat, scorching the bodily tissues. In severe cases, this can even consume the marrow, leading to the disease known as bone heat. If this persists, it can develop into bone steaming, causing fever in the afternoons, emaciation, red cheeks, dry mouth, fluctuating heat both day and night, nocturnal sweats, anxiety in the five hearts, lethargy in the limbs, reduced appetite, and lingering toxins after recovery, which can be treated with Sheng Xi San.

    Regarding spontaneous sweating: It is said that infants up to one year old should not experience spontaneous sweating. If they do, it can deplete yang energy, leading to a weak pulse and scattered spirit, potentially provoking wind-stroke. For any spontaneous sweating in infants and young children, do not stop it abruptly. It is advisable to use 2.5 qian of Bai Zhu and a pinch of wheat, boil to dryness, grind the wheat into powder, and decoct with Huang Qi soup to administer, treating until fully recovered. If there is spontaneous sweating due to typhoid fever, it should be treated with Xiao Chai Hu Decoction with Long Dan added. In summer, when spontaneous sweating is common, it is appropriate to use Bai Hu Decoction to manage it. In cases of excessive heat leading to spontaneous sweating and wheezing, Ge Gen Huang Lian Huang Qin Decoction is recommended.[3p]
    Zheng Zhi Zhun Sheng 【Ming Wang Kentang】

    Sweat

    Sweat is the essence stored in the heart; when it is within, it forms blood, and when it is exuded, it becomes sweat. Sweat is the liquid of the heart; thus, when a person's qi and blood are balanced, they are at peace, but when imbalanced, they suffer from illness. The classics say: when yin is deficient, yang must rise, leading to fever and spontaneous sweating; when yang is deficient, and yin prevails, it causes coldness and spontaneous sweating—both results stem from an imbalance between yin and yang.

    Children have delicate and weak blood and their skin pores are not tightly closed; if they are dressed too warmly, steam will invade their organs, generating heat in the organs that attacks the heart, thus overpowering the fluids, leading to sweat on the skin, known as night sweats. Also, if they are affected by cold or heat, with alternating effects causing disharmony between yin and yang, the fluids might leak out, resulting in sweat during sleep.

    There are indications of deficiency and excess: deficiency refers to conditions after severe sweating, where the blood and qi remain weak, leading to fluid overflow and spontaneous sweating, either with tidal fever or after experiencing cold or heat, resulting in coolness with spontaneous sweating. Over time, this can lead to yellowing and emaciation, and if not treated, it may turn into fever and wasting. DanXi states: night sweats refer to sweating while sleeping; if awake, there is no sweating. When deeply asleep, sweat emerges slowly; upon waking, it stops and does not return, which is also a sign of heart deficiency. It is advisable to conserve heart qi and nourish kidney fluids to harmonize yin and yang, allowing the water and fire to balance, thus stopping the sweating. Qian's principle states: from the head down to the neck is known as the six yang deficiency sweat, which does not require treatment.[4p]
    Self-sweating occurs when sweat emerges without waiting for an external prompt. The classics state: when one overeats, sweat arises from the stomach; when startled, sweat comes from the heart; during heavy long journeys, sweat is produced by the kidneys; from rapid running and fear, sweat originates from the liver; from physical strain and labor, sweat issues from the spleen. It is further said: when yin is deficient and yang must respond, sweat arises with heat; when yang is deficient and yin must prevail, sweat appears with symptoms of collapse. Dong Yuan mentioned: for self-sweating due to exterior deficiency, use cinnamon in autumn and winter, and astragalus in spring and summer. Danxi remarked: sweat is the fluid of the heart. There has never been a case of self-sweating that did not arise from deficiency in both the heart and kidneys. The Chao family stated: in deficiency consumption illness, if yang is particularly weak, fluids may leak out as self-sweating. The heart is the primary yang organ, associated with fire. Yang governs yin, and the fluids of the body arise where yang energy is present and leak where fire disturbs; hence, this leads to self-sweating. The treatment should involve ginseng, astragalus, sweet, warm herbs to strengthen yang externally and contain fluids internally, thus stopping the sweat. If the original qi is deficient, in summer, use the Six Gentlemen Decoction with added yam and cornelian cherry; in winter, add or reduce to Ba Wei Wan or the Ten Complete Vitality Decoction. For blood deficiency, use the Four Substances Decoction with ginseng and astragalus. Those with excess heat should take the Angelica and Six Yellow Decoction.[5p]
    For those with both qi and blood deficiency, use Shiquan Dabu Decoction. For heart and kidney deficiency with heat, use Liuwei Dihuang Pills. For deficiency with cold, use Bamei Pills. For heart channel blood deficiency, use Tuan Shen Decoction. For stomach channel qi deficiency, use Liu Junzi Decoction. For dietary fatigue, use Buzhong Yiqi Decoction. For those who crave sleep and feel fatigued, use Shengyang Yigui Decoction. For heat injuring original qi, use Qingzao Decoction. For summer heat affecting the pericardium, use Qingshu Yiqi Decoction. For external wind evil, use Xingxing Powder. For wasting and emaciation, use Ren Shen Yangrong Decoction. For thinking and worrying damaging the spleen, use Guipi Decoction. For anger stirring liver fire, use Xiao Chai Hu Decoction. For liver channel deficiency heat, add flavors to Xiao Yao San. For liver channel damp-heat, use Long Dan Xie Gan Decoction. For diarrhea with weak pulse, use Ren Shen Li Zhong Decoction. For sweating in hands and feet, use Buzhong Yiqi Decoction. For sweating in the chest and abdomen, use Si Junzi Decoction. For sweating specifically from the heart area, use Fuling Buh Xin Decoction. For yellow sweat, use Yinchen Wuling San. For blood sweating, apply Xue Yu San. These are all major methods for dealing with sweating. Additionally, consider the interplay of the five organs. If sweating is like oil, accompanied by incessant wheezing, this indicates a dire situation; if soft sweat is yellow, this indicates spleen failure; if sweat appears without flowing, like pearls, this indicates cessation of sweat. The frequent cases are not treatable. If there is deficiency in all six yangs, sweating will rise to the head and descend to the neck, indicating difficulty in treatment.[6p]
    Children with spleen deficiency experience spontaneous sweating, often from the forehead, which can cling to hands. Quickly bolster the stomach qi using the whole scorpion powder; prepare a decoction with ginger and jujube for adjustment and also use the sandalwood drink for treatment.

    For spleen deficiency leading to spontaneous sweating that is cold all over, this can sometimes occur during diarrhea but may subside without it. After diarrhea, the condition manifests; this situation is one of great deficiency and requires urgent spleen replenishment with remedies like Yi Huang San, Shen Ling Bai Zhu San, and Fuzi Lizhong Decoction.

    For lung deficiency characterized by spontaneous sweating, the right side of the face often appears pale and the lung pulse feels weak. This typically arises from prolonged coughing, with persistent sound, minimal phlegm that is not viable, leading to lung meridian deficiency and obstruction of qi which results in sweating. It is advisable to use the Lung Replenishment Powder for treatment, along with the Huoxiang Drink to adjust the spleen, as this also signifies a mother's intent to care for a child.

    In cases of chronic fright leading to spontaneous sweating throughout the body, which feels icy, the condition is dangerous; Golden Liquid Pill and Guozhen Decoction are the primary treatments.

    For spontaneous sweating due to an external cause, such as contracting wind evil leading to fever, resulting in intermittent consciousness and profuse sweating, it is important to rescue the exterior and relieve muscle tension using the Baijiesan, brewed in water for consumption; alternatively, the Wuling Powder may be used along with warm white soup for adjustment.

    When the stomach is weak and sweating extends from the nape down to the navel, this indicates stomach deficiency and requires stomach supplementation, with Yi Huang San being suitable. For Yang deficiency manifesting as sweating from the nape that does not extend beyond the chest, no treatment is necessary.[7p]
    For excessive sweating, when one is heavily clothed and sweating from the forehead, the prescription is the Chih Huan San. Hai Zang states: A child has suffered from night sweats for seven years, and all medicines have been ineffective. I prescribed Liang Ge San and San Huang Wan, and the illness was resolved in three days. The essence of the kidneys transforms into the five bodily fluids, which become the five types of dampness. The upward movement of the fire of the kidney forces the kidney water to ascend, taking advantage of the emptiness of the heart and entering the hand's Shao Yin. The heart fire is intense and ascends to the lungs, overwhelming them. Consequently, the skin opens, and the pores and original orifices do not close, leading to sweating. Sweating during sleep is termed "night sweats," as one does not sweat when awake; hence the classic text states: "Night sweat despises wind." First, use Liang Ge San to drain the fire from the chest; as the fire recedes, the next step is to use San Huang Wan to drain the heart fire to aid the yin, thereby allowing kidney water to return to the original organs, closing the orifices, and stopping the sweating.

    For children without illness who sweat profusely while sleeping, feeling as if the body is drenched, and remain wet for a long time after waking, this is termed "accumulated condition of night sweats," caused by the coldness of the spleen. Use San Ling San, decocted with ginger water, followed by Yi Huang San and Shen Ling Bai Zhu San.

    At times, if there is slight cold sweat, especially noticeable at the hair roots like beads, and the forehead and face are moist, this indicates a "startled liver condition." It is advisable to use Zhen Jing Wan, or Hu Po Bao Long Wan, alongside Fu Shen Tang, adding Ephedra root, to decoct and take for effect.[8p]
    Night Sweating

    When one sleeps, sweat comes out, and when one is awake, it recedes. Qian said: If a child sweats during sleep, it indicates weakness of the muscles and flesh; use the Stop Sweat Powder for treatment. If there is sweating all over the body, use the Perfumed Melon Pill for treatment. If the sweating occurs from the chest down to the navel, it indicates weakness of the stomach; one should tonify the spleen and use the Beneficial Yellow Powder for treatment. Spontaneous sweating belongs to Yang deficiency, while night sweating belongs to Yin deficiency. Yang corresponds to the defensive Qi, and Yin corresponds to the nourishing blood. Blood primarily resides in the heart and is stored in the liver. When heat attacks the heart, fluids cannot be contained within and instead leak out through the skin. When a person sleeps, they are calm and in Yin state; when they awaken, they are active and in Yang state. Hence, we say spontaneous sweating pertains to Yang and night sweating to Yin. This often results from the heart and kidney not communicating, with water and fire unable to harmonize. Kidney deficiency causes loss of containment, leading to this condition. It is advisable to use the Six Flavors Pill or the Perfectly Harmonized tonifying decoction. For blood deficiency with internal heat, use the Angelica and Six Yellow Decoction. If there is heat in the heart channel, use the Red Guiding Powder. For deficiency heat in the liver channel, use the Six Flavors Rehmannia Pill. For blood loss causing night sweating, use the Angelica Blood-Tonifying Decoction. If there is wind-heat in the liver and gallbladder, use the Chai Hu Clearing Liver Powder. For food accumulation and internal heat, use the Two Aged, Bitter Orange, and Gardenia. For stomach Qi deficiency with heat, use the Six Gentlemen Decoction and Floating Wheat Powder. If both blood and Qi are deficient, use the Ginseng Nourishing Glory Decoction. Other conditions regarding spontaneous sweating should be referred to for further guidance.

    If there is sweating that occurs during sleep at night, it is called night sweating, which is caused by Yin deficiency. If it persists for a long time, it can lead to emaciation and weakness, insufficient heart Qi, and excessive loss of body fluids. Use the Poria Decoction with added Astragalus, and take it with jujubes boiled with ginger and salt.[9p]

    Prolapse refers to the enlargement of the yin organ falling down and failing to retract. It is treated with concoctions of Mu Tong, Gan Cao, Huang Lian, Dang Gui, and Huang Qin. For prolapse, a powder of Perilla leaves mixed with water is applied topically and wrapped with lotus leaves.

    Pediatric Diseases Exposition (Ming Dynasty, Wan Quan)

    (Nèití) Hernia

    　　　　　阴肿疝气

    　　巢氏云：足少阴为肾之经，其气下通于阴。小儿有少阴之经虚而受风邪者，邪气冲于阴，与血气相搏结，则阴肿也。然儿有大小壮弱，起止中节可也。苟肾经气虚，或坐石不起，冷气凝之，或近地经久，风邪湿气伤之，不为阴肿者，几希矣。间有啼叫怒气，闭击于下，给聚不散，加以水窦不行，亦能发为此疾。治法，桃仁丸。如小儿外肾肿大，茎物通明，牡蛎粉研极细，鸡子清调敷为佳。又有疝气名偏坠，急宜下药。小儿生下亦有如此者，不疼不痛，此皆不须攻击，不治而自愈。若肿痛甚，急当服药，宜五苓散、青木香丸、汤氏化生丸，并疏气药。《济生方》：牡丹皮、防风为末，酒调服，极效。五苓散加防风、牡丹皮亦效。此证有热甚者，三黄丸治之。[19p]

    　　小儿阴肿疝气者，多属肝肾气虚，及坐卧寒湿之地，或风邪所伤，血气相搏，或啼叫气逆，水道不行，或禀父肝经虚热，或妊娠肝气郁结，或乳母怒动肝火而致者。若儿肝经热，用栀子清肝散。儿啼躁怒，用匀气散。乳母恚怒，用柴胡清肝散。肝火气逆，用加味逍遥散。小腹作痛，小便濇滞，用龙胆泻肝汤。久坐冷地，小便不利，用四苓散加柴胡、山栀、车前子。不时寒热者，加味小柴胡汤。经云：肝气热则茎痿，宗筋弛纵，肾茎肿胀，或出白液痒痛，或里急筋缩，挺纵不收，或精随便下者，此名筋疝，俱属肝火，不系于肾，宜详治之。[20p]
    　　　　证治准绳 【明　王肯堂】

    　　　　　疝

    　　《内经》大奇论曰：肾脉大急沉，肝脉大急沉，皆为疝证。心脉搏滑急为心疝，肺脉沉搏为肺疝。盖疝者寒气结聚之所为，故令脐腹绞痛者是也。又巢元方曰：诸疝者，阴气积于内，复为寒气所伤，荣卫不调，二气虚弱，风冷入腹而成。故《脉经》云：急者紧也，紧则为寒、为实、为痛。血为寒泣则为瘕，气为寒聚则为疝，皆因本脏气虚，外感于寒湿，内伤于生冷，遂使脐腹绞刺，激搏而痛，无有定处，仓卒之际不堪忍者，谓之疝也。[21p]
    并宜先用五苓散，沸汤调服和解。轻则但以白芍药汤、乌梅散、钓藤膏为治；重者金茱丸、散气丸，未有不愈也。　　(疒颓)疝者，阴核气结，肿大而钓痛也，多因小儿啼怒不止，动阴气，故阴气下系，结聚不散而得之；或胎妇啼泣过伤，令儿生下，小肠气闭，亦变此证。惟是阴气不得流行，加以风冷入焉，白水聚焉，故水气上乘于肺，先喘急而后疝痛。其状有如李者，亦有稀软者，亦有并肾肿大者，亦有大硬者，脐下痛楚，皆不能忍。用药行心气，逐肾邪，利其大小二便，更无补法。

    　　　　　阴肿

    　　小儿阴肿核肿者，由儿啼怒气逆不顺，乘虚而行，阴核偏大；又因甘肥不节，生冷过度，致生疳气，气结不行，流入阴中；或伤暑毒，或触风邪，使血气与邪气相搏，停结不散，则成阴肿也。　　若肾经气虚，或坐石不起，冷气凝之，或近地经久，风邪湿气伤之，不为阴肿几希矣。间有啼叫，怒气闭系于下，结聚不散，加以水窦不行，亦能发为此疾。治用桃仁丸主之。丹溪谓脱囊肿大，坠下不收，用紫苏茎叶为末，水调荷叶包之。一人传此方，用野白紫苏为末，湿则掺之，干则香油调傅，虽皮溃子坠，皆有神效。此用紫苏，盖亦同功也。　　诸筋会于阴器，邪客于厥阴少阴之经，与冷气相搏，则阴囊肿痛而引缩。经中虽分四证：曰肠(疒颓)、气(疒颓)、水(疒颓)、卵(疒颓)，然小儿患此，若治之不早，则成痼疾。[23p]
    如腰曲腹痛，冷汗自出，而阴囊二子吊缩入腹，痛止方出，名为内吊，用乌梅散、匀气散、金茱丸、金铃散为治。　　有阴茎全缩不见，有阴囊光肿不痛，此因肝肾气虚，宜以橘子仁煎汤，调下金铃散、匀气散，皆可投之。《内经》曰：(疒颓)癃疝肤胀者，阴亦盛而脉胀不通，故曰(疒颓)癃疝。由是观之，乃阴气盛而致有此。吊缩者，筋急也。筋遇寒则引缩，遇热则弛张，故《三因》所用方法，以宽小肠气疏风为治。然小儿此证，多因坐阴润之地，感风湿而得。[24p]
