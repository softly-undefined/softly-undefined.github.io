Thousand Gold Prescriptions (by Sun Simiao of the Tang Dynasty)[1p]

Erythema

Erythema, also known as "heavenly fire," appears as a sudden red coloration in the flesh resembling vermilion. The larger patches can be as big as a palm, while in severe cases, it can cover the entire body. It may be itchy and swollen, with an unpredictable hue. There exists a condition called "blood vermilion," originally termed "white vermilion," according to "Qianjin" Volume 22, which describes swelling in the flesh that is itchy with abdominal pain, slightly swollen as if blown up, resembling a rash. There is also "cockscomb erythema," which is red and raised, with larger patches like coins and smaller ones like millet grains, presenting a texture on the skin resembling a chicken’s comb, and is also called "cornel erythema." "Watery erythema" arises due to generalized body heat, and when it comes into contact with water, it forms yellow-red spots resembling bubbles, often found in skin regions like the thighs and groin. Although this may seem like a minor ailment, if left untreated, it can lead to death.

Urticaria

According to "Su Wen," when wind evils invade the skin, it results in the skin becoming weak and the true Qi dispersing, further affected by cold, causing an opening of the pores and hair follicles, leading to chaotic movement of excess Qi, which results in itchiness. Thus, both wind rash and itchiness are caused by this. There are also red rashes that suddenly appear as if bitten by mosquitoes, causing severe itchiness, and in exacerbated cases, they become hugely raised, resulting in further scratching. There are also white rashes exhibiting similar characteristics. Red rashes appear with heat and subside with cold, while white rashes emerge in cool, overcast weather. For white rashes, it is advisable to wipe them with boiled alum juice or to bathe in water mixed with a bit of wine. Alternatively, wiping with boiled heather juice is also beneficial. Treatment for others should be similar to that of erythema. Commonly referred to as "wind droppings," it is also known as "wind corpse."[2p]

Dan Poison

The internal poison (Dan) appears as redness and swelling from beneath the ribs to the lower back, and it is known as internal poison. If noticed early, it can be treated; however, once it reaches the waist, treatment is no longer possible. This illness often results in difficulty urinating and defecating, and it can lead to a rapid spread of a dark, blue-black coloration across the body, resulting in death within three days. For urgent treatment, apply medicinal powders directly to the affected areas and take Lianqiao Baidu Powder.

The external poison (You Dan) is characterized by widespread red poison across the body. Initially, it manifests as bluish discoloration in both hands, with swelling throughout. If it goes untreated for a night or two, the swelling can spread throughout the body; if it enters the heart, it leads to death. At the first sign, urgent treatment is necessary, which includes taking Five-Spice Lianqiao Decoction and Mu Xiang Liuqi Drink, along with the application of Jin Guo Powder. It is advisable to treat it using methods similar to those for carbuncles.

Children suffering from red wandering fire poison often acquire heat in the womb from their mothers, which leads to symptoms appearing on the skin in a migratory manner.[3p]
If the illness originates from the abdomen and moves outward to the limbs, it is considered light; if it originates from the limbs and returns to the abdomen, it is heavy, and urgent treatment will be necessary for survival. The children's condition, known as "red fire toxin," arises from accumulated heat, which is termed "fetal poison." This can occur if the mother during pregnancy consumes spicy and toxic foods, bathes in hot water, or uses charcoal in winter, allowing heat to enter the fetus, along with excessive cravings. Some children may be born wearing warm clothing or sleeping on heated bedding, leading to inner and outer heat toxins attacking the body—most will show symptoms within about six months. Initially, the body will feel hot; if observed with light, it will appear red. If the child cries uncontrollably and has an unstable appearance, those showing symptoms in the limbs are likely to survive, while those with symptoms in the waist and abdomen are unlikely to survive.

Immediate action must be taken: use a sharp container to remove the purple blood, allowing the toxic blood to flow downward—do not reverse the procedure. Apply powdered frankincense mixed with egg white to the affected area, and periodically apply juice from banana roots. Internally, administer a detoxifying pill made from vermilion mixed with honey, and follow it with a decoction made from "purple gold" preparation.[4p]

Discussion on Treatment

Fire poison arises from excessive heart fire and the wind-heat of the triple burner, which manifests on the surface of the skin. It may present differently in terms of dryness and wetness, as well as variations in color. In dry cases, the color is red, resembling cloud fragments, with wind blisters that itch and feel hot. This pertains to the fire of the heart and liver meridians, treated by cooling the heart and draining the liver, using the "Transforming Rash Detoxifying Decoction." In wet cases, the color is more yellowish-white, with varying sizes that ooze and cause pain. This relates to the damp-heat of the spleen and lung meridians, warranting treatment to clear the lungs, drain the spleen, and eliminate dampness, using the "Wei Ling Decoction." If the rash arises in the lower back and flanks, it is due to excessive liver fire, known as “waist wrap fire poison,” treated with "Chai Hu Qing Gan Decoction," with external application of cypress leaf powder or "Ruyi Jinhuang San."
The rash can be triggered by toxins encountered before birth, manifesting after birth.[16p]
The body in the womb relies on the father's essence and the mother's blood for nourishment. If the parents cannot restrain their desires, and the passion turns rampant, the fetus will inevitably be affected. Additionally, if they do not avoid rich flavors and are exposed to smoke and heat from cooking, or if they are bundled in heavy blankets, the fetus often suffers from overheating, which can be passed on to the child. After birth, if the baby is washed with hot water and the clothes are exposed to heat, internal toxins can be triggered, leading to the baby feeling heat first, crying out in alarm, and experiencing swelling; this can manifest as redness and heat that moves unpredictably, often starting from the forehead, known as "Tian Zhao Dan," treatable with Shengma and Geggen decoctions for both mother and child. If symptoms arise from the abdomen and back, they are lighter; those starting from the limbs and affecting the chest and abdomen are heavier. The key is to first treat the bad blood. After treating the blood, slices of fresh pig meat should be applied to the affected area, routinely replaced with a golden powder mixed with water and banana juice, preferably changed twice daily. Internally, use drinks like Dalianqiao decoction, Xieduoxiang decoction, and Wufu detoxifying pills. If toxins cause the abdomen to swell and harden, leading to an inability to nurse, Zixue powder can be administered. After three days, the body gradually cools down, the treated areas become soft and active, sounds are clear, the abdomen softens, and nursing returns to normal. Conversely, if these symptoms do not resolve, it indicates a setback.[17p]
Bumps and Rashes

The condition of bumps and rashes, though already well-documented in standard texts, generally refers to children's measles. However, in cases where adults or children suddenly develop bumps or rashes that are not measles, while similar, there are indeed slight differences that must be distinguished for proper treatment. This is often caused by external wind-heat.

The treatment method: if the pulse is floating and there is a fever with exterior symptoms, the primary focus should be on dispersing the wind pathogen. If the pulse is floating and rapid, clear the heat while expelling wind; if the pulse is deep and slippery without exterior symptoms, focus on clearing fire. If the pulse is both floating and deep, slippery and rapid with symptoms present both internally and externally, it is advisable to treat both the exterior and interior simultaneously.

Moreover, this condition is more commonly seen in children, and one must assess their internal and external state of deficiency and excess to determine treatment. Overall, since children are fragile, more nourishing medicines should be used while less aggressive methods are preferable. If symptoms are secretive, mild dispersal is appropriate; if they are constricted, gentle guidance is appropriate. The aim is to ensure that the pathogenic qi does not stagnate but disperses easily, allowing the condition to be lighter and more stable. In general, warmth in the body indicates a beneficial direction, while coolness suggests a harmful one.[18p]

　　　　　斑疹丹毒

　　斑疹一证，虽已有正门详载，然彼以小儿麻疹为言，其有非麻疹而无论大人小儿，忽患斑疹小疮者，此虽与彼相类，而实有小异也，是亦不可不辨而治之，盖多由风热外感之证耳。治此之法，脉浮而身热，有表证者，惟散风邪为主。脉浮而数者，祛风兼清热；脉沉滑而无表证者，清火为主；脉浮沉俱滑数而表里兼见者，宜表里双解之。然惟小儿多有此证，须察其表里虚实，酌而治之可也。总之，小儿脆弱，宜安里之药多，攻发之药少，秘则微泄之，结则微导之，但令邪气不壅而散之易，则证轻而自安矣。大抵身温暖者顺，身凉者逆。[18p]

Erythema

Erythema, also known as "heavenly fire," appears as a sudden red coloration in the flesh resembling vermilion. The larger patches can be as big as a palm, while in severe cases, it can cover the entire body. It may be itchy and swollen, with an unpredictable hue. There exists a condition called "blood vermilion," originally termed "white vermilion," according to "Qianjin" Volume 22, which describes swelling in the flesh that is itchy with abdominal pain, slightly swollen as if blown up, resembling a rash. There is also "cockscomb erythema," which is red and raised, with larger patches like coins and smaller ones like millet grains, presenting a texture on the skin resembling a chicken’s comb, and is also called "cornel erythema." "Watery erythema" arises due to generalized body heat, and when it comes into contact with water, it forms yellow-red spots resembling bubbles, often found in skin regions like the thighs and groin. Although this may seem like a minor ailment, if left untreated, it can lead to death.

Urticaria

According to "Su Wen," when wind evils invade the skin, it results in the skin becoming weak and the true Qi dispersing, further affected by cold, causing an opening of the pores and hair follicles, leading to chaotic movement of excess Qi, which results in itchiness. Thus, both wind rash and itchiness are caused by this. There are also red rashes that suddenly appear as if bitten by mosquitoes, causing severe itchiness, and in exacerbated cases, they become hugely raised, resulting in further scratching. There are also white rashes exhibiting similar characteristics. Red rashes appear with heat and subside with cold, while white rashes emerge in cool, overcast weather. For white rashes, it is advisable to wipe them with boiled alum juice or to bathe in water mixed with a bit of wine. Alternatively, wiping with boiled heather juice is also beneficial. Treatment for others should be similar to that of erythema. Commonly referred to as "wind droppings," it is also known as "wind corpse."[2p]
