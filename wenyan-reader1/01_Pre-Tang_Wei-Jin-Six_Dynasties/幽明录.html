    In Shixing, there is a spring called Ling Water, which has a hot spring at its source. Whenever frost or snow comes, the steam rises tens of feet high. If you throw living things into it, they will cook in a moment. There are often tiny red fish swimming in the spring, but no one catches them.

    In Aixian, Fushan has two hot and cold springs, both originating from the base of the same mountain. The two springs originate from sources a few feet apart. The hot spring can cook chickens and pigs, and the cold spring is always like ice. The two streams flow for several meters before merging into one stream.

    In Xiangyi County's Nanxie Township, this is the old hometown of Laozi. There is a Laozi temple, and within the temple are nine wells. Those who enter the temple after purifying themselves will find the water warm and clear according to their thoughts.

    In Shian Xiping County in the southeast, there is a mountain. The western side of the mountain is long and narrow, and water flows down into a pond, which rises and falls twice a day, hence it is called "Chaoxi Pond."

    In Leiyang County's northeast, there is a reed pond, covering eight hectares, and its depth is immeasurable. There are large fish in the pond that leap out of the water every five days, reaching a circumference of three zhang, and their appearance is extraordinary. Each time they leap out of the water, small fish scatter and follow the water onto the shore, too many to count.

    Between Yidu and Jianping counties, there are five or six peaks, alternating in height. On top of these peaks, there are leaning stones that resemble two people facing each other with their sleeves raised. Locals say that the magistrates of the two counties disputed the boundary here.

    On the northern mountain of Wuchang Yangxin County, there is a stone called the "Wife-Watching Stone," which looks like a person standing. It is said that once there was a chaste woman whose husband went off to serve in the military, far away to fight a national crisis. The woman accompanied her weak child to this mountain to see him off. She stood there watching for her husband, and later turned into a standing stone, which is why it is named as such.

    Bachu County, twenty li above Jin Gang, is known as the Golden Gorge, whose depth is immeasurable; above it is a rapid, also called the Golden Rapid. In ancient times, a fisherman who fished in this gorge caught a golden chain. When he pulled it, it filled an entire boat. Then a golden bull emerged, its voice and appearance fierce and mighty. The fisherman was terrified, and the bull, braving courageously, leapt back into the gorge. The chain was nearly used up, and the fisherman cut off several feet of it with a blade. The gorge and rapid thus received their names.

    In Niu Zhu Jin of Huainan, the waters are extremely deep, beyond calculation. People have seen a golden bull of most magnificent form, bound with a golden chain.

    From Lu Mountain, traveling south for more than ten li, there is Chicken Mountain, which has a stone resembling a chicken, with a comb and spurs as if alive. The Daoist priest Li Zhen lived below this mountain and often treasured and admired the stone. One day, the stone suddenly collapsed, and Li Zhen told others, "If the chicken has suddenly changed like this, is my end near?" He then took leave of his close friends and acquaintances, and within a month thereafter he died.

    The Three Peaks are the most towering and lofty; unless on a clear morning after a storm, they cannot be seen. Beneath the peak is a spring, whose waters cascade like a piece of silk unfurling, reflecting through the green forest and flowing straight down to the mountain's base. Though the fine silk remains still, above it rustles gently, always accompanied by a cold, clear wind.

    By the side of Gongting Lake, nestled among the hills, there are several stones, round in shape like mirrors, clear enough to reflect a person's image, called the Stone Mirror. Later, a traveler passed by and set fire to one of them, and it was never again clear. The traveler then lost his sight.
