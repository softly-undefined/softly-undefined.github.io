    夏至，曰未中，少许在所住户南床眠。始觉，仍令善生下帘。又眠未熟，忽见一人，长可七尺，面小，口鼻猛，眉多，少有须，青白色，年可四十许。著朱衣赤帻，上戴蝉垂，缨极长，紫革带广七寸许，带鞶囊，鞶囊作龙头。足著两头舄，舄紫色，行时有声，索索然。从者十二人，二人提裙，作两髻髻，如永嘉老姥髻，紫衫，青裤，履缚裤极缓。三人著紫裤，褶平巾帻，手各执简，简上有字，不可识。又七人，并白布裤褶，自履褶悉有所执，一人挟坐席，一人把如意五色毛扇，一人把大卷书，一人持纸笔大砚，砚黑色，笔犹如世上笔，一人捉伞，伞状如毛羽，又似彩帛，斑驳可爱。伞形圆深，柄黑色，极长。入屋后，倚檐前。其二人并持囊，囊大如小柱，似有文书。挟席人舒置书床上，席白色，有光明，草缕如■〈艹邪〉子，但伞缕尤大耳。侍者六人入户，并倚子平床前，此人始人户，便皵面去，居太近，后仍就座，以臂隐书案。

    On the summer solstice, it was still early in the day, and I had just begun to feel awake, still ordering Sheng to lower the curtain. I was not yet fully asleep again when I suddenly saw a person, approximately seven feet tall, with a small face, prominent mouth and nose, many eyebrows, a few mustaches, a pale or bluish-white complexion, and around forty years old. He was wearing red clothes and a red headdress, adorned with a cicada pendant, a very long tassel, a purple leather belt about seven inches wide, and a pouch shaped like a dragon's head. His shoes were two-toed and purple, making a rustling sound when he walked. He was accompanied by twelve attendants: two holding up his skirt, with a hairstyle resembling that of an elderly woman from Yongjia, wearing purple shirts and blue pants, with shoes tied loosely; three wearing purple pants and a flat cap, each holding a tablet with writing that could not be recognized; seven more, all wearing white cloth pants and jackets, each holding something: one carrying a sitting mat, one holding a five-colored feather fan, one holding a large scroll, one holding paper, pen, and a large inkstone, the inkstone black and the pen similar to a common pen, one holding an umbrella resembling feathers and colorful silk, with a speckled and attractive appearance, the umbrella round and deep, with a black handle that was very long. After entering the house, he leaned against the eaves. Two others held bags, which were as large as small pillars and seemed to contain documents. The person carrying the mat spread it out on the bookshelf. The mat was white and luminous, with threads like grass. But the threads of the umbrella were particularly large. Six attendants entered the house and leaned against the bed. This person, upon entering, wrinkled his face and moved closer, then sat down and hid his arm behind the desk.

    At that time, the brush and measuring tool were all on the desk. He then took them and placed them inside the compartment, moving the compartment to the northern end. He asked his attendants, "Why didn't you bring the table?" They replied, "You were recently traveling, so we didn't bring it." He then said to Zi Liang, "I am the deputy of this mountain office. I commend you for having no wrongdoing, so I have come to visit you." Zi Liang then rose and adjusted his robe, but had not yet answered. He continued to ask, "Today is a good day, the day is favorable, have you fasted?" Zi Liang replied, "I eat lunch as usual, I have not yet understood the method of fasting." He said again, "Lunch is sufficient, but sleeping in summer is not beneficial, do not always be greedy for sleep." Zi Liang replied, "My body is weak, even small matters make me feel tired, the tiredness is as if I want to sleep, and I cannot restrain myself." He said, "Minor matters are of no harm." As the wind rose, it blew the umbrella nearly over, and he still ordered his attendants to look at the umbrella. Red beans came into the courtyard, dangling to the edge of the umbrella, and the attendants used their hands to push them away.

One of them asked, "Where did this little boy come from?" Ziliang answered, "I am from Qiantang, my surname is Yu, and I am temporarily staying here." He then said, "Do not let him be naked." A good spirit saw this and asked, "Who is Lang Shan?" Ziliang answered, "I am from Yongjia, and I am staying under the protection of Mr. Tao."     As for living as a human, one is indeed attached to the world; after death, one becomes a spirit and lingers in the underworld. Speaking frankly, the underworld is superior. There is a vacancy in the office, and I wish to appoint you to fill it. The matter is nearly decided, so do not speak further. In October next year, I will summon you, so prepare accordingly. I am informing you of this now. If you do not obey this command, the three officials' edict will come. You must be cautious about it. I immediately showed a look of fear. This person said, "It seems you want to remain in the world and commit sins. Why should you be appointed to a position in my cave? Facing the true nature, the sacred court, considering the world, there is no place better than this." Subhuti then said, "I will follow your instructions." He also said, "Since childhood, you have not been without minor faults. You should reflect and repent. If you do not, it will also burden you. All who practice the Way do not expose their bodies or hair, wrong the innocent, and follow the rules in all actions and meals. I have told you all I can. Now that you have taken on the position, you still have doubts, but it is not far away. Take my words to heart and do not reveal them to the world. Those who are indifferent, those in the mountains who share the same breath, will not be offended if they know."

    便下席，未出户，见门上有令春、刘白等（令春是姨母间裸子，刘白是白从子），乃又曰：“勿令小儿辈逼坛靖，靖中有真经，前失火处，大屋基，今犹有吏兵防护，莫轻洿慢。其辈无知，事延家主，卿姨病源乃重，虽不能致毙，亦难除。”子良因问：“不审若为治疗？股中又有结病，何当得除？”答曰：“不可即除，岁月之间不知若为耳。腹中亦有卒可差，别当向卿言。”令春等去，便下阶而灭。

    　　二十七曰二更中，开眼见一人，在床前，容质端正，有须，须甚厚，细眉目，年可二十余，颜状甚可爱。著芙蓉冠，垂青缨甚长，著衣状如单衣，而有朱青黄白相杂厕，似锦复非素。腰带不知是何所著，亦有光，如前范帅来时烛光也。独自而已，自云是中山人，因言曰：“茅君用尔为丞，已遣丞帅来相报。事已定，吾今来教尔修道之方。可从而言疏。”子良仍起，襞纸疏之。

    He quickly descended from the mat, not yet out the door, and saw on the door Mr. Lingchun, Mr. Liu Bai, and others. Lingchun is the son of the aunt, and Liu Bai is the grandson of Bai. He then said, "Do not let the young ones approach the altar of tranquility. The altar contains true scriptures. The place where there was a fire before, the foundation of the large house, is still guarded by officials and soldiers. Do not treat lightly or disrespect. They are ignorant, and their actions will affect the master of the house. Your aunt's illness is severe, though it may not be fatal, it is difficult to cure." Zi Liang asked, "I wonder how to treat it. There is another lump in the leg. When will it be removed?" He replied, "It cannot be removed immediately; it is unclear what will happen over time. There is also a sudden improvement in the abdomen, which I will tell you about separately." After Lingchun and others left, he descended the steps and disappeared.

    On the twenty-seventh day, during the second watch of the night, he opened his eyes and saw a person in front of the bed. The person had a dignified appearance, a beard, a thick beard, and fine eyebrows and eyes. He was around twenty years old, and his appearance was very lovely. He wore a peony cap with long green tassels. His clothes were like a simple robe, with red, blue, yellow, and white mixed together, resembling brocade but not plain. The belt was unknown, but it also had light, similar to the candlelight when the previous commander came. He was alone. He said he was from Zhongshan. He then said, "Master Mao has appointed you as a deputy and has already sent a deputy commander to inform you. The matter is settled, and I am here to teach you the way of cultivation. You can write it down." Zi Liang then stood up and wrote it down.
